## 1. Purpose

**Avantajul Uman** helps professionals strengthen the human skills that remain irreplaceable in an age of automation and AI: critical thinking, adaptability, creativity and sound judgement.

The program focuses on how humans think, learn and decide when faced with ambiguity, incomplete information and rapidly changing contexts. It is not about resisting technology, but about becoming the kind of professional AI cannot replace.

**Scope statement:**
This program is **not training in AI tools, communication techniques, conflict management or strategic planning**. It develops the underlying human capabilities — thinking, judgement, creativity and adaptability — that support all other professional skills. If participants need those specific skills, they should be referred to PMD_001–PMD_004.

---

## 2. Challenge solved

| Problem | Consequence |
|---|---|
| Professionals rely on templates or algorithms without questioning them | Poor decisions, missed risks, blind spots |
| People struggle to adapt when plans change | Stress, rigidity, lost opportunities |
| Teams generate many ideas but few good ones | Superficial innovation, wasted effort |
| Judgement is confused with opinion or gut feeling | Inconsistent decisions, weak justification |
| Fear of AI displacement reduces confidence and initiative | Disengagement, underinvestment in human skills |

The program solves these by developing the thinking habits that enable people to navigate complexity with confidence and creativity.

## 3. Program promise

> Ceea ce te face om rămâne de neînlocuit.

**What the promise means in this program:**

Participants leave able to:
1. **Question** assumptions and information instead of accepting them passively.
2. **Adapt** their approach when the situation changes.
3. **Generate** original, useful ideas under constraints.
4. **Judge** wisely when data is incomplete or conflicting.
5. **Combine** human insight with technological tools without becoming dependent on them.

**Example:** A marketing manager receives an AI-generated campaign plan. Instead of accepting it, she asks: *„Ce audiență a fost presupusă? Ce date lipsesc? Ce valori ale brandului nu apar aici?"* She adapts the plan, adds creative elements AI missed and defends the final direction with clear reasoning.

Participants will leave the program able to:
- Apply structured critical thinking tools to real problems.
- Recognise cognitive biases and reduce their impact on decisions.
- Use creativity frameworks to produce novel solutions.
- Adapt quickly to new information or changing conditions.
- Explain and defend their judgement with reasons and evidence.

## 5. Learning outcomes

By the end of the program, participants will be able to:

1. **Identify** assumptions, evidence and reasoning in a given argument or proposal.
2. **Apply** at least 3 critical thinking tools to evaluate a real-world claim or problem.
3. **Recognise** common cognitive biases and explain how they affect judgement.
4. **Generate** multiple creative solutions to a constrained problem using a structured framework.
5. **Adapt** a plan or approach in response to new, unexpected information.
6. **Evaluate** the quality of a decision using criteria beyond immediate outcome.
7. **Create** a personal development plan for strengthening human judgement over 30 days.
8. **Explain** the complementary roles of human judgement and AI in professional work.

### Learning outcomes ↔ assessment matrix

| LO | Assessment method | Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| LO1 Identify assumptions / evidence / reasoning | Argument Anatomy worksheet | Correctly labels the three elements in a case |
| LO2 Apply 3+ critical thinking tools | Critical thinking toolkit exercise | At least 3 tools applied with short notes |
| LO3 Recognise cognitive biases | Bias Mitigation Checklist exercise | Identifies 3+ biases and mitigation strategies |
| LO4 Generate creative solutions | Creativity framework exercise | Produces 3+ distinct, feasible ideas |
| LO5 Adapt to new information | Adaptability simulation | Revised plan with rationale |
| LO6 Evaluate decision quality | Decision-quality case analysis | Evaluation using process, criteria and alternatives |
| LO7 Create 30-day development plan | Personal development plan template | Completed plan with focus area and weekly actions |
| LO8 Explain human–AI complementarity | Short written reflection or debate (max 250 words) | Clear distinction between human and AI roles |

## 7. Program structure

### Module 1 — Gândirea în era AI (60 min)
**Practical hook (first 20 min):** Participants receive a short AI-generated recommendation and must identify what is missing: context, values, trade-offs, uncertainty. They discuss what a human must add.

- Ce poate AI-ul și ce nu poate (15 min)
- De la informație la înțelegere: diferența dintre date și sens (15 min)
- Introducere în gândirea critică ca obicei profesional (10 min)

### Module 2 — Unelte de gândire critică (90 min)
- Anatomia unui argument: afirmație, dovezi, raționament, ipoteze
- Tehnici: 5 De Ce, analiza cauzelor, testul adversarului (devil's advocate)
- Exercițiu: deconstruirea unei propuneri din propriul domeniu folosind Argument Anatomy Template

### Module 3 — Biasuri și judecata (60 min)
- Biasuri comune: confirmare, ancorare, disponibilitate, prezență, optimism
- Cum să reducem impactul biasurilor: structuri, checklists, diversitate de perspective
- **Bias Mitigation Workshop (40 min):**
  - Participants receive a sector-specific decision scenario with embedded biases.
  - In pairs, they identify at least 3 biases and complete the Bias Mitigation Checklist.
  - Deliverable: completed checklist with specific mitigation actions for each bias.

### Module 4 — Creativitatea sub constrângere (60 min)
- De la brainstorming la gândirea convergentă
- Framework-uri: SCAMPER, „Ce-ar face dacă...", design constraints
- Exercițiu: generarea de soluții pentru o problemă reală cu resurse limitate

**Boundary with PMD_004:** This module focuses on generating ideas and creative thinking, not on selecting strategic direction or leading change. Decision and prioritisation belong to PMD_004.

### Module 5 — Adaptabilitatea și învățarea (60 min)
- Ciclul OODA: observă → orientează-te → decide → acționează
- Cum să rămâi stabil în valori și flexibil în metodă
- **Adaptability Simulation (40 min):**
  - Teams receive a plan for a project or intervention.
  - During the simulation, new information arrives twice that invalidates key assumptions.
  - Teams must revise their plan and present the revised approach and rationale.
  - Deliverable: revised plan with changes highlighted and reasoning explained.

**Boundary with PMD_003:** This module addresses adaptability as a thinking and learning skill, not emotional regulation or de-escalation under pressure. Those belong to PMD_003.

### Module 6 — Plan personal: Avantajul Uman în 30 de zile (30 min)
- Identificarea unei zone de dezvoltare: gândire critică, creativitate, adaptabilitate, judecată
- Primul experiment și modalitate de reflecție
- Resurse de continuare

## 9. Assessment

### Formative assessment
- Observation of argument analysis and creative exercises
- Peer feedback on judgement case studies
- Module-end quick checks

### Summative assessment
- **Practical deliverable:** One completed Argument Anatomy + Bias Mitigation Checklist + revised Adaptability Plan + judgement case evaluation.
- **Reflection brief:** 150–250 words on the most important human skill the participant will strengthen in the next 30 days and how.

### Practical deliverable rubric — Human judgement exercise

| Criterion | Excellent (4) | Good (3) | Acceptable (2) | Needs revision (1) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Critical analysis** | Clearly identifies assumptions, evidence and reasoning | Mostly clear | Some elements identified | Superficial or missing |
| **Bias awareness** | Recognises and mitigates relevant biases using checklist | Recognises biases | Limited awareness | No awareness |
| **Creativity** | Produces novel, feasible ideas within constraints | Mostly feasible | Few or obvious ideas | No relevant ideas |
| **Adaptability** | Revises approach thoughtfully when information changes | Revises with some reason | Minor revision | Rigid |
| **Judgement quality** | Decision is well-reasoned and criteria-based | Mostly reasoned | Weak justification | Opinion only |
| **Reflection** | Clear personal insight and commitment to practice | Insight present | Surface | Absent |

### Success indicators
- 100% of participants submit Argument Anatomy, Bias Mitigation Checklist, revised Adaptability Plan and judgement case evaluation.
- 90% of participants report increased awareness of their own thinking habits.
- 80% of participants identify at least one area where they will rely more on human judgement than on templates or tools.

### 10.3 Human Edge Checklist + Human–AI Complementarity Matrix

**Human Edge Checklist**

Before accepting a recommendation, plan or decision, ask:
- [ ] What assumptions are hidden here?
- [ ] What evidence supports this and what evidence is missing?
- [ ] Could a bias be influencing me or the source?
- [ ] What values or ethical issues are not visible?
- [ ] What would a reasonable opponent say?
- [ ] Have I considered at least two alternatives?
- [ ] What is the human cost or impact?
- [ ] What must I add that a machine cannot?

**Human–AI Complementarity Matrix**

| Task | AI is better at | Humans are better at |
|---|---|---|
| Processing large data sets | Yes | No |
| Detecting patterns in data | Yes | Sometimes |
| Asking the right question | No | Yes |
| Interpreting context and values | No | Yes |
| Generating novel ideas | Sometimes | Yes |
| Evaluating ethics and impact | No | Yes |
| Adapting to unwritten rules | No | Yes |
| Explaining and defending judgement | No | Yes |
| Building trust and relationships | No | Yes |
| Knowing when data is not enough | No | Yes |

### 10.5 Bias Mitigation Checklist

| Bias suspected | Evidence of bias in this situation | Mitigation action I will take |
|---|---|---|
| Confirmare | I only see information that supports what I already believe | Actively seek one source that disagrees |
| Ancorare | I am stuck on the first number / idea I heard | Generate 2–3 fresh estimates or alternatives |
| Disponibilitate | I am relying on vivid but unrepresentative examples | Look for base-rate data or broader sample |
| Prezență | I overvalue what is in front of me | Ask what is missing from the picture |
| Optimism | I assume things will go better than evidence suggests | Consider the worst credible scenario |

## 11. Sector adaptations

### Education
- **Focus:** questioning information, encouraging student curiosity, adapting teaching methods, creative problem-solving in the classroom.
- **Note:** teachers model the thinking habits they want students to develop.

#### Example scenario: Education
A teacher receives a highly recommended AI-generated lesson plan. It is efficient but does not fit the specific needs of her class. She must decide what to keep, adapt or reject.

**Thinking focus:** identify assumptions in the AI output, apply criteria for student learning, adapt creatively, justify the final plan.

#### Advanced scenario: Education
A school is considering replacing part of its assessment with an AI grading tool. The tool is fast but cannot evaluate creativity or effort. The teacher must advise the leadership team.

**Judgement focus:** identify what AI can and cannot assess, evaluate the risk to student development, propose a hybrid model, defend the recommendation with criteria.

### Healthcare
- **Focus:** interpreting clinical data, recognising bias in diagnosis, adapting treatment plans, creative problem-solving under constraints.
- **Note:** clinical decisions remain with qualified professionals; this program supports the thinking process behind complex cases.

#### Example scenario: Healthcare
A medical team receives conflicting test results and patient history. They must decide whether to follow standard protocol or pursue a less common diagnosis.

**Thinking focus:** weigh evidence, recognise availability bias, consider alternatives, explain judgement clearly to the team and patient.

#### Advanced scenario: Healthcare
A hospital considers using an AI diagnostic support system that recommends treatments based on population data. A patient does not fit the typical profile. The clinician must decide whether to follow the AI recommendation or override it.

**Judgement focus:** identify limitations of population-based data, evaluate patient-specific factors, explain the override with clinical reasoning, document the decision.

### Public Administration
- **Focus:** evaluating policy evidence, recognising political bias, adapting to citizen needs, creative solutions with limited resources.
- **Compliance note:** reasoning must be transparent, evidence-based and aligned with public interest.

#### Example scenario: Public Administration
An official must evaluate a popular but unproven policy proposal that is gaining media traction. The evidence is thin but the political pressure is high.

**Thinking focus:** separate evidence from rhetoric, identify assumptions, consider unintended consequences, make a defensible recommendation.

#### Advanced scenario: Public Administration
A city wants to use predictive analytics to allocate social services. The algorithm flags a neighbourhood as high-risk, but residents and social workers disagree with the prediction.

**Judgement focus:** question the data model and its assumptions, evaluate fairness and context, involve stakeholders, decide whether and how to use the prediction.

### Business / SMEs
- **Focus:** evaluating market claims, recognising bias in strategy, adapting to disruption, creative product and process innovation.
- **Note:** link strong thinking to competitive advantage and resilience.

#### Example scenario: Business
A team is presented with a market research report that confirms what the leadership already believes. The team must decide whether to trust it or challenge it.

**Thinking focus:** identify confirmation bias, test assumptions, seek disconfirming evidence, propose alternative interpretations.

#### Advanced scenario: Business
A company uses an AI hiring tool that shortlists candidates with profiles similar to past high performers. The team notices the tool excludes diverse candidates.

**Judgement focus:** examine the algorithm's assumptions, evaluate the definition of "success", propose changes, defend a fairer process.

### NGOs
- **Focus:** evaluating impact claims, adapting programmes to changing contexts, creative fundraising and advocacy, ethical judgement.
- **Note:** human judgement protects mission integrity and beneficiary trust.

#### Example scenario: NGO
An NGO must evaluate an impressive external evaluation report on a new intervention before deciding to scale it. The data looks promising but the context differs from the NGO's work.

**Thinking focus:** examine methodology, identify assumptions, assess transferability, adapt or reject based on evidence and values.

#### Advanced scenario: NGO
An NGO is offered a donation from a company whose values conflict with the NGO's mission. The funding would solve immediate cash-flow problems.

**Judgement focus:** identify hidden assumptions and long-term consequences, evaluate mission alignment, consider creative alternatives, make an ethical decision transparently.

### 15.2 Facilitator requirements

**Essential:**
- Demonstrated experience in training adults in critical thinking, creativity, learning or cognitive skills.
- Strong facilitation skills for reflective, open-ended discussions.
- Ability to challenge assumptions without being dismissive.
- Capacity to keep the program focused on human cognition, not therapy, tools or strategy consulting.

**Desirable:**
- Background in education, healthcare, public administration, business or NGOs.
- Experience delivering both online and in-person experiential learning.
- Completion of the AnimaMinds Avantajul Uman facilitator induction.

**Facilitator ratio:** 1 facilitator per 18 participants; 2 facilitators for groups over 18 or where deep reflection is expected.

### 15.3 Evaluation methodology

**Pre-program (T0):**
- Critical thinking self-assessment (5 items, Likert 1–5)
- Creativity and adaptability self-assessment (5 items)

**During program:**
- Facilitator observation checklist
- Peer feedback on argument analyses and case studies
- Module-end quick checks

**Post-program (T1, within 48h):**
- Repeat self-assessments
- Argument Anatomy + Bias Mitigation Checklist + revised Adaptability Plan + judgement case evaluation submission
- Reflection brief
- Satisfaction survey (5 items)

**Follow-up (T2, 30 days):**
- Short online survey: thinking habits practised, decisions improved, adaptations made
- Optional second reflection brief

### 15.4 Quality assurance process

1. **Content review cycle:** Program content is reviewed every 6 months or after significant updates in cognitive science, creativity research or AI capabilities.
2. **Facilitator debrief:** After each delivery, facilitators complete a 15-minute debrief form noting what worked, what did not and any group dynamics.
3. **Participant feedback analysis:** Feedback scores and comments are reviewed after every 3 deliveries.
4. **Update log:** All changes to the PMD are logged with date, reason and approver.
5. **Sector validation:** Sector-specific case studies are reviewed by at least one practitioner every 12 months.
6. **Safety audit:** Once per year, case studies are reviewed to ensure they do not require therapy, individual coaching or organisational diagnosis.
