## Detailed changes

### 1. Boundary Matrix

**What changed:**
- Added Section 10.10 with a 10-row matrix comparing PMD_001, PMD_002, PMD_003 and PMD_004.
- Added a "Rule of thumb for facilitators" to route programs by main need.
- Added boundary notes in Module 4 and Module 5.

**Why changed:**
- Review finding: boundary with other PMDs needed to be more explicit.
- Facilitators and CPD reviewers need an unambiguous reference.

**Review finding resolved:** Yes — finding 9 and priority action 1.

---

### 2. Missing templates

**What changed:**
- Added Stakeholder Map template (10.4) with influence, interest, role/need and involvement columns.
- Added Change Map template (10.5) with present state, desired state, gaps, first steps, resistance and progress indicator.
- Added Direction Pitch template (10.6) with 7 sections and 3-minute time limit.
- Added Decision Tools Quick Reference Card (10.3) summarising all 5 frameworks.

**Why changed:**
- Review finding: several referenced templates were not supplied.
- Templates are essential for practical application and assessment.

**Review finding resolved:** Yes — finding 8 and priority action 2.

---

### 3. Resistance Response Library

**What changed:**
- Added Section 10.7 with two resistance-response pairs for each sector:
  - Education
  - Healthcare
  - Public Administration
  - Business
  - NGO

**Why changed:**
- Review finding: a resistance response bank was missing.
- Helps participants prepare for real objections.

**Review finding resolved:** Yes — finding 8 and priority action 3.

---

### 4. Module 4 strengthened

**What changed:**
- Rewrote Module 4 to include a 40-minute Change Mapping exercise.
- Defined participant deliverable: completed Change Map with current state, desired state, gap, first 3 steps and top 2 resistance points.
- Linked the exercise to the Change Map template in Section 10.5.

**Why changed:**
- Review finding: Module 4 was at risk of being too theoretical.
- A concrete exercise ensures participants produce a usable deliverable.

**Review finding resolved:** Yes — finding 4 and priority action 4.

---

### 5. Advanced sector scenarios

**What changed:**
- Added one advanced scenario for each sector in Section 11:
  - **Education:** restructuring the timetable for interdisciplinary projects
  - **Healthcare:** reorganising patient flow after increased admissions
  - **Public Administration:** merging two departments
  - **Business:** pivoting business model after losing a major client
  - **NGO:** deciding whether to merge with another organisation

**Why changed:**
- Review finding: sector adaptations needed more depth.
- Advanced scenarios provide richer material for experienced participants.

**Review finding resolved:** Yes — finding 6 and priority action 5.

---

### 6. Next-Day Clarity Sprint

**What changed:**
- Added a one-page 15-minute sprint in Section 10.3.
- Six steps: Name it, Map stakeholders, Choose a tool, Generate options, Pick criteria, Decide and draft the message.

**Why changed:**
- Review finding: a next-day toolkit could be more explicit.
- Supports immediate application after the program.

**Review finding resolved:** Yes — finding 3 and priority action 6.

## Verification of core focus

**Strategic clarity, prioritisation, decision-making and change leadership remain the core focus.**

Evidence:
- Section 1 (Purpose): explicitly about finding clarity, setting direction and making better decisions.
- Section 3 (Promise): operationalised around naming, mapping, choosing, aligning and acting.
- Section 5 (Learning outcomes): LO2 frameworks, LO3 stakeholders/criteria, LO4 change analysis, LO5 resistance, LO6 direction, LO7 action plan, LO8 decision timing.
- Section 7 (Program structure): six modules covering clarity, prioritisation, decision frameworks, change, direction communication and action planning.
- Section 11 (Sector adaptations): every scenario focuses on leadership decisions, not communication mechanics, conflict or AI.

**AI, communication/feedback and conflict/de-escalation remain in their respective PMDs.**

Evidence:
- Section 10.10 Boundary Matrix explicitly assigns AI tools to PMD_001, feedback/listening/communication mechanics to PMD_002, conflict de-escalation/emotional regulation to PMD_003.
- Module 4 boundary note states PMD_004 does not teach communication skills or conflict de-escalation.
- Module 5 boundary note states PMD_004 focuses on direction content, not communication mechanics.
- No module in PMD_004 teaches AI usage, active listening, feedback, conflict de-escalation or emotional regulation techniques.
