# Internal Review: PMD_001_AI_Fara_Haos

**Review date:** 2026-07-12  
**Reviewer:** Cascade  
**Status:** Draft — PMD_001 not modified; review only

---

## Executive summary

PMD_001_AI_Fara_Haos is a solid, practical foundation for a non-technical AI literacy program. The document is clear, well-structured and aligned with the approved AnimaMinds portfolio positioning. However, before it can serve as a master template or support a CPD submission, several sections need strengthening: assessment evidence, facilitator guidance, sector-specific content, and CPD-aligned learning hours/credits.

**Overall readiness:** 7 / 10 — good draft, needs refinement before approval.

---

## 1. Is the program promise clear?

**Verdict:** Yes, with a minor gap.

The promise `Folosește AI. Păstrează controlul.` is concise and memorable. The four bullet points that follow explain the promise operationally. What is missing is a clearer definition of „control” — does it mean data control, quality control, decision control, or all three? A one-sentence expansion would help.

**Suggested improvement:** Add a clarifying line such as: *„Control înseamnă să decizi tu ce taskuri delegi AI-ului, să verifici outputul și să rămâi responsabil pentru rezultatul final.”*

**Score:** 8 / 10

## 3. What can a participant do the next day after completing the program?

**Verdict:** Clear and practical.

A participant can:
- Use a structured prompt to draft an email, lesson plan, report or meeting summary.
- Evaluate an AI-generated draft for accuracy, tone and bias.
- Edit AI output to match their own voice.
- Apply the privacy checklist before entering any information into an AI tool.
- Identify at least three repetitive tasks to delegate to AI.

**Suggested improvement:** Add a „Next-day toolkit" section summarising the exact steps and templates.

**Score:** 8 / 10

---

## 4. Are there any modules that are too theoretical?

**Verdict:** Module 1 risks being too theoretical.

| Module | Risk | Recommendation |
|---|---|---|
| Module 1 — Demistificarea | Medium-high | Keep it to 60 minutes, not 90. Start with a live demo in the first 10 minutes. |
| Module 2 — Identificarea taskurilor | Low | Already exercise-based |
| Module 3 — Prompting | Low | Highly practical |
| Module 4 — Aplicații practice | Low | Should be the heart of the program |
| Module 5 — Verificare și editare | Low | Practical |
| Module 6 — Plan personal | Low | Practical |

**Suggested improvement:** Add an activity in Module 1 where participants test a simple prompt themselves within the first 20 minutes.

**Score:** 7 / 10

---

## 5. Are there overlaps between modules?

**Verdict:** Yes, minor overlaps exist.

- **Module 1 and Module 5:** Both discuss limits, ethics and verification. The content is related but justified — Module 1 introduces limits, Module 5 applies them to editing.
- **Module 2 and Module 4:** Both involve identifying tasks. Module 4 should focus on *applying* prompts to role-specific tasks, not re-identifying them.
- **Module 3 and Module 4:** Module 4 is the natural continuation of Module 3; the boundary is clear but must be enforced in facilitator notes.

**Suggested improvement:** Add a facilitator note for each module clarifying what is *not* covered there and what belongs to the adjacent module.

**Score:** 7 / 10

## 8. Which resources are missing?

**Missing resources identified:**

1. **Facilitator training guide** — how to handle resistance, fear, or over-enthusiasm about AI.
2. **Sample completed deliverables** — examples of good, acceptable and excellent outputs for assessment.
3. **Troubleshooting guide** — what to do if AI tools are slow, blocked or produce harmful content.
4. **Data privacy consent / disclaimer template** — for use with organisations.
5. **Follow-up email sequence** — reminders at 7, 14 and 30 days post-program.
6. **CPD certificate template** — if the program becomes accredited.
7. **Accessibility notes** — how to adapt for participants with visual impairments or low digital literacy.
8. **Technical fallback plan** — offline activity options if internet fails.
9. **Evaluator rubric** — for the practical deliverable.

**Score:** 6 / 10

---

## 9. Which sections need improvement before CPD submission?

**Critical gaps for CPD:**

1. **Learning hours / CPD credits** — no statement of total learning hours or CPD point equivalence.
2. **Facilitator qualifications** — not documented.
3. **Evaluation methodology** — needs pre/post confidence scale, knowledge check and 30-day follow-up data.
4. **Quality assurance** — no process for reviewing and updating content as AI tools evolve.
5. **Learning outcomes vs. CPD criteria** — outcomes are not yet mapped to any recognised competency framework.
6. **Participant feedback loop** — no mechanism described for using feedback to improve the program.
7. **Equality, diversity and inclusion statement** — missing.
8. **Cancellation and substitution policy** — missing (relevant for paid / accredited delivery).

**Suggested improvement:** Add a CPD appendix with the above elements.

**Score:** 5 / 10

## Priority actions before approval

1. **Shorten Module 1** to 60 minutes and add a hands-on demo in the first 20 minutes.
2. **Add a Learning Outcomes / Assessment matrix** mapping each LO to an observable task.
3. **Add CPD appendix** with learning hours, facilitator qualifications, evaluation methodology and QA process.
4. **Add sector-specific example prompts and completed deliverables** as an appendix.
5. **Add missing resources**: facilitator troubleshooting guide, sample deliverables, follow-up email sequence, data privacy template.
6. **Clarify the meaning of „control"** in the program promise.
7. **Add facilitator notes** to prevent module overlap.

---

## Conclusion

PMD_001 is a strong draft that can become the master template. It is practical, audience-aware and aligned with the approved portfolio. The main gaps are CPD readiness, facilitator support materials and sector-specific depth. Addressing the priority actions above will bring the document to approval-ready status.
