## 1. Facilitator role

You are not a therapist, counsellor or mediator. You are a skills facilitator who helps participants:
- Recognise early signs of escalation in themselves and others.
- Regulate their emotional state in real time.
- De-escalate tense interactions without avoiding the issue.
- Set boundaries and know when to refer.

Your job is to build practical calm-under-pressure skills while protecting participant safety and program scope.

## 5. Module scripts

### Module 1 — Ce se întâmplă sub presiune (60 min)

**Objective:** Participants can recognise physiological and behavioural signs of escalation.

**Slides 2–6**

**Hook (20 min):**
- 0–5 min: Present a short high-pressure scenario video or reading.
- 5–15 min: Identify the exact moment the professional lost control.
- 15–20 min: Practise a 60-second grounding technique together.

**Content (30 min):**
- Slides 7–10: Pressure-reaction cycle — stimulus, interpretation, emotion, reaction.
- Slides 11–15: Signs of escalation in body, voice, words, behaviour.
- Slides 16–20: From reaction to response.

**Time check at 40 min:** Hook and content complete.

**Consolidation (10 min):** Workbook Section 1 and 2.

---

### Module 2 — Autoreglarea emoțională (60 min)

**Objective:** Participants can apply at least 3 self-regulation techniques.

**Slides 21–28**

**Mini-lesson (15 min):**
- Slides 21–24: Techniques — breathing, anchoring, distancing, emotional labelling.
- Slides 25–28: Choosing a personal "base" technique.

**Activity: Regulation Lab (40 min)**
- 0–5 min: Introduce technique cards.
- 5–30 min: Participants test 3 techniques and rate effect.
- 30–40 min: Share which technique worked best and why.

**Time check at 30 min:** All three techniques tested.

**Deliverable:** Workbook Section 3 completed.

---

### Module 3 — Anatomia unui conflict (60 min)

**Objective:** Participants can analyse conflict using positions, interests, needs (PIN).

**Slides 29–36**

**Mini-lesson (20 min):**
- Slides 29–32: PIN framework.
- Slides 33–36: Levels of conflict intensity.

**Activity: PIN Mapping (30 min)**
- 0–5 min: Present a sector scenario.
- 5–20 min: Participants complete PIN map in pairs.
- 20–30 min: Debrief commonalities and possible solutions.

**Deliverable:** Workbook Section 4 completed.

---

### Module 4 — Strategii de de-escaladare (90 min)

**Objective:** Participants can choose and apply de-escalation strategies.

**Slides 37–45**

**Mini-lesson (25 min):**
- Slides 37–40: Verbal techniques — redirect, reframe, partial validation, clear limits.
- Slides 41–45: Non-verbal techniques and phrase bank.

**Activity: De-escalation Rewrite (55 min)**
- 0–10 min: Present reactive responses on cards.
- 10–30 min: Rewrite responses in pairs.
- 30–45 min: Pressure Role-Play in triads (professional, other party, observer).
- 45–55 min: Debrief using Calm Response Rubric.

**Time check at 30 min:** Rewrites complete.

**Deliverable:** Workbook Section 5 completed.

---

### Module 5 — Limite, siguranță și referire (60 min)

**Objective:** Participants can set boundaries and identify when to refer.

**Slides 46–52**

**Mini-lesson (20 min):**
- Slides 46–48: Saying "stop" professionally.
- Slides 49–52: Referral Decision Tree.

**Activity: Boundary Script (30 min)**
- 0–10 min: Participants choose a boundary situation.
- 10–25 min: Write a 4-sentence boundary script.
- 25–30 min: Pair practice.

**Debrief (10 min):**
- What makes a boundary feel firm but respectful?
- When would you involve HR, security or counselling?

**Deliverable:** Workbook Section 7 completed.

---

### Module 6 — Plan personal de calm sub presiune (30 min)

**Objective:** Participants create a 30-day calm-under-pressure plan.

**Slides 53–55**

**Activity (20 min):**
- 0–5 min: Introduce Workbook Section 9.
- 5–15 min: Complete 30-Day Calm Plan.
- 15–20 min: Pair share of trigger, technique and first step.

**Closing (10 min):**
- Each participant states one commitment.
- Remind of follow-up email sequence.
- End with promise: *„Pauză. Respirație. Reconstrucție."*

## 6. Closing script

**Time:** 5 minutes  
**Slide 56:** Closing slide

> Am ajuns la final. În următoarele 30 de zile veți primi patru emailuri care vă vor ajuta să rămâneți în practică.
>
> Trei întrebări înainte să plecați:
> 1. Care este triggerul pe care îl veți observa cel mai atent?
> 2. Care este tehnica voastră de bază?
> 3. Cui veți cere ajutor dacă situația depășește programul?
>
> Amintiți-vă: calm sub presiune nu înseamnă lipsa emoțiilor. Înseamnă să alegi cum răspunzi.
>
> Pauză. Respirație. Reconstrucție. Mulțumesc.

## 7. Assessment guide

### 7.1 Practical deliverable

Each participant submits one rewritten high-pressure response + one boundary script.

Use the PMD rubric:

| Criterion | Excellent (4) | Good (3) | Acceptable (2) | Needs revision (1) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Self-regulation signal | Clear pause or grounding before action | Brief pause implied | No visible regulation | Escalatory reaction |
| Clarity | Calm, specific and unambiguous | Mostly clear | Vague or emotional | Confusing or hostile |
| De-escalation | Strategy fits situation and reduces intensity | Reduces intensity | Neutral effect | Escalates or avoids |
| Boundaries | Sets or maintains limits respectfully | Limits present but soft | Limits unclear | Aggressive or absent |
| Referral awareness | Knows when to involve others | Mentions possible referral | Unclear | Ignores need for support |

### 7.2 Success indicators
- 100% submit rewritten response and boundary script.
- 90% report increased confidence in handling pressure.
- 80% can name at least 2 self-regulation techniques they will use within 30 days.

---

## 10. Troubleshooting

| Issue | Response |
|---|---|
| Participant overwhelmed by scenario | Pause, offer observer role or break. |
| Group minimises conflict | Use real, anonymised sector cases. |
| Participant wants to control others | Reframe: we regulate ourselves and influence, not control. |
| Existing team conflict surfaces | Stay in scope: individual skills and team protocols. |
| Scenario too role-specific | Use generic pressure scenarios plus sector breakouts. |

## Appendix A — Scenario Cards

### Card 1: Educație
**Situație:** Un părinte intră furios în cancelarie și acuză profesorul că a notat incorect copilul.
**Rol:** Profesorul rămâne calm și clarifică faptele.
**Focus:** Validare parțială, reformulare, pasul următor.

### Card 2: Sănătate
**Situație:** Un pacient așteaptă de o oră și începe să țipe la asistentă.
**Rol:** Asistenta gestionează situația cu calm și siguranță.
**Focus:** Siguranța pacientului, limite, redirectionare.

### Card 3: Administrație publică
**Situație:** Un cetățean refuză să accepte un răspuns negativ și devine tot mai tare.
**Rol:** Funcționarul aplică limite clare și redirecționează.
**Focus:** Proceduri, transparență, încheiere conversație.

### Card 4: Business
**Situație:** Un coleg reproșează public unui manager că a pierdut un client.
**Rol:** Managerul răspunde calm, fără să contraatace.
**Focus:** Redirecționare, soluție, întâlnire ulterioară.

### Card 5: ONG
**Situație:** Un voluntar insistă să ia o decizie care încalcă procedura.
**Rol:** Coordonatorul stabilește limita și oferă pasul următor.
**Focus:** Misiune, procedură, respect.
