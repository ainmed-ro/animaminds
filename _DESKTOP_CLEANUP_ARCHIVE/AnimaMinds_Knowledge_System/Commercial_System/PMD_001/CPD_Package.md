## 5. Facilitator qualifications

### Essential
- Demonstrated experience in training adults in a professional context.
- Practical experience using generative AI tools in their own work.
- Ability to explain technical concepts in non-technical language.
- Familiarity with data privacy and AI ethics basics.

### Desirable
- Background in education, healthcare, public administration or business (to match sector adaptations).
- Experience delivering both online and in-person training.
- Completion of the AnimaMinds AI Fără Haos facilitator induction.

### Facilitator ratio
- 1 facilitator per 20 participants.
- 2 facilitators for groups over 20.

**Rationale:** The standard 1:20 ratio applies for AI literacy sessions where the main activities are individual or pair-based and emotional intensity is moderate. A second facilitator is added for larger groups to support technical troubleshooting and observation.

## 6. Assessment and evaluation methodology

### 6.1 Completion criteria

To receive a certificate and CPD points, participants must:
- Attend at least 80% of the program contact hours.
- Submit the practical deliverable (one AI-assisted work asset) within 48 hours after the program.
- Submit the reflection brief (150–250 words) within 48 hours after the program.
- Complete the post-program satisfaction survey.

Participants who do not meet these criteria may submit a short remediation assignment within 14 days, subject to facilitator review.

**Appeals:** Participants who believe their assessment was unfair or incorrect may submit a written appeal to the AnimaMinds QA Lead within 14 days of receiving the assessment decision. The appeal will be reviewed by a second assessor, and the outcome will be communicated within 14 days.

### 6.2 Pre-program (T0)

**Confidence questionnaire (5 items, Likert 1–5)**
1. I feel confident using generative AI tools.
2. I know when AI can help my work.
3. I can write a clear prompt.
4. I know how to check AI outputs for accuracy.
5. I understand the privacy risks of using AI.

**Current AI use self-assessment**
- How often do you currently use AI in your work?
  - Never / Rarely / Monthly / Weekly / Daily
- What is your main barrier to using AI?
  - Time / Skills / Trust / Access / Policy / Other

### 6.3 During program

- Facilitator observation checklist
- Participant worksheet completion
- Module-end quick checks

### 6.4 Post-program (T1, within 48h)

**Repeat confidence questionnaire**
- Same 5 items as T0.

**Practical deliverable submission**
- One AI-assisted work asset relevant to the participant's role.
- Submitted via form or email.

**Reflection brief (150–250 words)**
- What will you use AI for?
- What will you not use AI for?
- How will you maintain control?

**Satisfaction survey (5 items, Likert 1–5)**
1. The program met my expectations.
2. The facilitator explained concepts clearly.
3. The activities were practical and relevant.
4. I feel more confident using AI after the program.
5. I would recommend this program to a colleague.

### 6.5 Follow-up (T2, 30 days)

**Short online survey**
1. How many tasks have you delegated to AI since the program?
2. What is one concrete time-saving result?
3. What challenges have you encountered?
4. Have you shared the privacy checklist with a colleague?
5. Will you continue using AI in your work?

Optional: submission of a second deliverable for additional feedback.

## 9. Quality assurance process

1. **Content review cycle:** Program content is reviewed every 6 months or after any major AI tool update, whichever comes first.
2. **Facilitator debrief:** After each delivery, facilitators complete a 15-minute debrief form.
3. **Participant feedback analysis:** Feedback scores and comments are reviewed after every 3 deliveries.
4. **Update log:** All changes to the PMD and commercial assets are logged with date, reason and approver.
5. **Sector validation:** Sector-specific examples are reviewed by at least one subject-matter expert every 12 months.
6. **Data privacy audit:** Once per year, the privacy checklist and tool recommendations are reviewed.

## 11. Ethical and safety guardrails

- Participants are instructed never to enter personal, confidential or sensitive data into public AI tools.
- AI is framed as a support tool, not an author or decision-maker.
- All outputs must be verified, edited and approved by a human.
- Participants are encouraged to check their organisation's AI-use policy.
- Facilitators correct misuse live and use mistakes as learning moments.
