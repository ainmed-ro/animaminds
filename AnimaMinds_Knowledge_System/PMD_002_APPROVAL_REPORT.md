## Detailed changes

### 1. Facilitator emotional safety protocol

**What changed:**
- Added Section 10.5 with step-by-step guidance for facilitators when a participant becomes upset.
- Included specific language to use without crossing into therapy or counselling.
- Added a protocol for disclosures of trauma, mental health crisis or abuse.
- Added facilitator self-care guidance.

**Why changed:**
- Review finding: emotional safety resources were missing.
- The program deals with sensitive topics; facilitators need clear boundaries and a safe referral path.

**Review finding resolved:** Yes — review finding 8 and priority action 1.

---

### 2. Clarification of LO6 and evidence

**What changed:**
- Reworded LO6 from "Repair a working relationship after a difficult conversation" to "Identify concrete repair steps for a working relationship after a difficult conversation, and describe them in a written plan."
- Updated the assessment matrix to specify evidence: "3–5 concrete, respectful, actionable steps described in writing."
- Clarified that live repair is not required; a written reflection or repair script is sufficient evidence.

**Why changed:**
- Review finding: LO6 was hard to observe in real life.
- Live relationship repair cannot be required or measured within the program timeframe.

**Review finding resolved:** Yes — review finding 2 and priority action 2.

---

### 3. Observer roles and chat-based alternatives

**What changed:**
- Added a "Observer roles for online and on-site role-play" subsection in Section 8.
- Defined a three-person structure: Speaker, Recipient, Observer.
- Added a "Chat-based alternative for online role-play" for participants who do not want to use video or voice.

**Why changed:**
- Review finding: online role-play needed alternatives.
- Observer roles increase participation and learning while reducing performance anxiety.

**Review finding resolved:** Yes — review finding 7 and priority action 3.

---

### 4. Active Listening Assessment Rubric

**What changed:**
- Added Section 9.2 with a 5-criterion rubric for active listening.
- Criteria: Paraphrase, Open questions, Validation, Summarising, Non-verbal presence.

**Why changed:**
- Review finding: only the feedback rubric existed; listening was not explicitly assessed.
- Active listening is a core LO3 and must be observable and measurable.

**Review finding resolved:** Yes — review finding 8 and priority action 4.

---

### 5. Scope statement: not therapy, counselling or mediation

**What changed:**
- Added a prominent scope statement in Section 1.
- Added "not therapy, counselling or mediation" to the "Not included" audience list in Section 4.
- Included scope language in the emotional safety protocol.

**Why changed:**
- Review finding: CPD and safeguarding require a clear boundary statement.
- Protects participants, facilitators and the organisation from scope creep.

**Review finding resolved:** Yes — review finding 9 and priority action 5.

---

### 6. Advanced sector scenarios

**What changed:**
- Added one advanced scenario for each sector in Section 11:
  - **Education:** peer-to-peer teacher feedback about interrupting students
  - **Healthcare:** addressing dismissive communication between senior specialists
  - **Public Administration:** communicating a controversial policy change to citizens
  - **Business:** aligning two directors after a public disagreement in a leadership meeting
  - **NGO:** declining donor funding that would compromise beneficiary confidentiality

**Why changed:**
- Review finding: sector adaptations were good but could be deeper.
- Advanced scenarios provide richer material for experienced participants and facilitators.

**Review finding resolved:** Yes — review finding 6 and priority action 6.

## Comparison with planned PMD_004 Calm sub Presiune

The boundary between PMD_002 and PMD_004 has been verified and remains minimal.

| PMD_002 Conversații care Contează | PMD_004 Calm sub Presiune (planned) |
|---|---|
| Communication mechanics | Emotional regulation under pressure |
| Feedback and listening | Conflict escalation and de-escalation |
| Difficult conversations: clarity and respect | Difficult conversations: calm and professionalism under pressure |
| Alignment and collaboration | Resilience and self-regulation |
| One-to-one and team dialogue | High-stakes and conflict-rich situations |
| Not therapy/counselling/mediation | Not therapy/counselling/mediation |

**Overlap check:**
- Both programs address "difficult conversations."
- PMD_002 focuses on **what to say and how to listen**.
- PMD_004 focuses on **how to stay calm and regulated** while saying it.
- The Module 4 boundary statement in PMD_002 explicitly excludes conflict escalation, mediation and sustained pressure management.
- No content duplication risk has been identified.

**Recommendation:** Boundary is clear. Proceed with PMD_003 and PMD_004 development independently.
