# Internal Review: PMD_003_Calm_sub_Presiune

**Review date:** 2026-07-12  
**Reviewer:** Cascade  
**Status:** Draft — PMD_003 not modified; review only

---

## Executive summary

PMD_003_Calm_sub_Presiune is a strong first draft that follows the approved master template. It successfully shifts the focus to conflict management, emotional self-regulation, de-escalation and communication under pressure. The boundary with PMD_002 is clearly stated in Module 4 and the scope statement.

Main areas for improvement: preventing accidental overlap in the boundary statement, adding one more de-escalation activity, strengthening the referral section and tightening the sector examples.

**Overall readiness:** 7.5 / 10 — good draft, minor refinements needed before approval.

---

## 1. Is the program promise clear?

**Verdict:** Yes.

The promise `Pauză. Respirație. Reconstrucție.` is memorable and emotionally resonant. The five-point operational definition is clear and practical.

**Suggested improvement:** Add one short example of a before/after response under pressure to make the promise tangible.

**Score:** 8 / 10

## 3. What can a participant do the next day after completing the program?

**Verdict:** Clear and practical.

A participant can:
- Recognise early signs of pressure in their body.
- Use one grounding technique before responding.
- Reframe a reactive statement into a calm professional response.
- Set a boundary respectfully under pressure.
- Identify when to involve a manager, HR or security.

**Suggested improvement:** Add a "Next-day pressure response card" summary in Section 3 or 10.

**Score:** 8 / 10

## 5. Are there overlaps between modules?

**Verdict:** Minor overlap.

- **Module 1 and Module 2:** Both touch on physiological responses. The boundary is acceptable but could be sharper.
- **Module 4 and Module 5:** Both involve high-intensity situations. Module 4 focuses on de-escalation; Module 5 on stopping or referring.
- **PMD_002 boundary:** The boundary statement is present but could be reinforced with a concrete "not this" list.

**Suggested improvement:** Add a table in Section 13 or an appendix showing exactly what belongs to PMD_002 vs. PMD_003.

**Score:** 7 / 10

**Suggested improvement:** Add a boundary matrix and referral decision tree.
