## Detailed changes

### 1. PMD_002 ↔ PMD_003 Boundary Matrix

**What changed:**
- Added Section 10.6 with a clear matrix comparing PMD_002 and PMD_003 across 10 topics.
- Added a "Rule of thumb for facilitators": if the situation is calm enough for dialogue, feedback and listening, it belongs to PMD_002; if it is already heated, pressured or conflictual, it belongs to PMD_003.
- Added a boundary note in Module 4 clarifying that PMD_003 does not teach active listening, feedback or communication mechanics.

**Why changed:**
- Review finding: the boundary with PMD_002 was stated but needed to be more explicit.
- Facilitators and CPD reviewers need an unambiguous reference.

**Review finding resolved:** Yes — finding 5 and priority action 1.

### 2. Referral Decision Tree

**What changed:**
- Added Section 10.5 with a 6-step decision tree covering: immediate danger, ability to continue safely, workplace behaviour issue, need for mediation, mental health crisis, and uncertainty.
- Mapped outcomes to facilitator, manager, HR, mediation and counselling/specialist referral.

**Why changed:**
- Review finding: the referral section needed strengthening.
- Participant and facilitator safety require a clear escalation path.

**Review finding resolved:** Yes — finding 9 and priority action 2.

## Verification of primary focus

**Emotional self-regulation, pressure management and de-escalation remain the primary focus.**

Evidence:
- Section 1 (Purpose): explicitly about managing conflict, regulating emotions and communicating under pressure.
- Section 3 (Promise): operationalised around pause, regulate, de-escalate, respond, recover.
- Section 5 (Learning outcomes): LO2 self-regulation, LO4 de-escalation strategy, LO5 calm response, LO6 boundaries, LO8 referral.
- Section 7 (Program structure): six modules focused on pressure cycle, self-regulation, conflict anatomy, de-escalation, limits/referral and calm plan.
- Section 11 (Sector adaptations): every scenario focuses on pressure/conflict response, not feedback or listening mechanics.

**Feedback, active listening and communication mechanics remain in PMD_002.**

Evidence:
- Section 10.6 Boundary Matrix explicitly lists active listening, feedback and communication mechanics as core to PMD_002 and not taught in PMD_003.
- Module 4 facilitator note states that active listening, feedback and communication mechanics are assumed as prerequisites and not the focus.
- No module in PMD_003 teaches the feedback formula, active listening techniques or communication frameworks.
