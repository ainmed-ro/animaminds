## 1. Purpose

**AI Fără Haos** helps professionals understand, adopt and integrate generative AI into their daily work without losing control, authenticity or professional judgement.

The program transforms AI from a source of anxiety or hype into a practical, controllable tool that handles repetitive, time-consuming tasks so participants can focus on high-value human work.

---

## 2. Challenge solved

| Problem | Consequence |
|---|---|
| AI is perceived as complex, threatening or inaccessible | Professionals avoid it or use it ineffectively |
| Daily workflows include repetitive writing, summarising, formatting and research tasks | Time is consumed by low-value activity |
| Teams lack a shared language for discussing AI capabilities and limits | Misuse, over-reliance or inconsistent quality |
| Fear that AI will replace human roles | Resistance, anxiety, disengagement |
| No clear policy or ethical framework for AI use in the organisation | Risks around data privacy, copyright and misinformation |

The program solves these by offering practical, jargon-free AI literacy with immediate application.

---

## 3. Program promise

> Folosește AI. Păstrează controlul.

**What „maintaining control” means in this program:**

Participants remain in control when they:
1. **Decide** which tasks to delegate to AI and which to keep human-only.
2. **Verify** facts, sources and tone before using any AI-generated output.
3. **Edit** AI output so it reflects their own voice, expertise and context.
4. **Protect** confidential, personal or sensitive data by choosing what information enters an AI tool.
5. **Take responsibility** for the final result — AI is a support tool, not the author or decision-maker.

Participants will leave the program able to:
- Identify tasks that AI can accelerate or automate.
- Use AI tools effectively and ethically.
- Validate, edit and take responsibility for AI-generated outputs.
- Maintain their professional judgement and human voice.

### Primary audience

- Manageri
- Lideri de echipă
- Specialiști
- Antreprenori

### Secondary audience

### Not included

- Persoane care caută doar liste de prompturi fără înțelegere
- Organizații care vor să înlocuiască oameni cu AI

## 5. Learning outcomes

By the end of the program, participants will be able to:

1. **Explain** what generative AI can and cannot do in a professional context.
2. **Identify** at least 5 repetitive tasks in their own workflow that can be accelerated with AI.
3. **Use** core prompting techniques (role, context, format, constraints, iteration).
4. **Evaluate** AI outputs for accuracy, bias, tone and suitability.
5. **Edit** and personalise AI-generated content so it reflects their own voice and expertise.
6. **Apply** a simple ethical and data-privacy checklist before using AI.
7. **Create** at least one practical work asset using AI (email, lesson plan, report, presentation, etc.).
8. **Develop** a personal AI-use plan for the next 30 days.

### Learning outcomes ↔ assessment matrix

| LO | Assessment method | Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| LO1 Explain AI capabilities | Short knowledge check (5 questions) + group discussion | Correct answers + oral contribution |
| LO2 Identify 5 repetitive tasks | AI Audit worksheet | Completed worksheet with 5+ tasks |
| LO3 Use prompting techniques | Prompt Lab deliverable + Practical asset | Improved prompt drafts and final asset |
| LO4 Evaluate AI outputs | AI vs. Human comparison + Evaluation checklist | Annotated output with strengths/risks |
| LO5 Edit and personalise content | Practical deliverable (final version) | Deliverable edited from AI first draft |
| LO6 Apply ethical/privacy checklist | Ethical Dilemma Cards + Checklist sign-off | Completed checklist, no violations |
| LO7 Create one work asset | Sector Scenario Station output | Role-relevant asset submitted |
| LO8 Develop 30-day plan | 30-Day Action Plan template | Completed plan with first step defined |

## 7. Program structure

### Module 1 — Demistificarea AI-ului (60 min)
**Practical hook (first 20 min):** Participants open a free AI tool and complete a 3-minute challenge: „Scrieți un email scurt de scuză către un părinte/coleg/pacient/client.” They compare the AI draft with their own version and discuss what surprised them.

- Ce este și ce nu este AI-ul (15 min)
- Tipuri de AI: generativ, predictiv, analitic (10 min)
- Limitări reale și mituri comune (15 min)
- Etică, date personale și confidențialitate (15 min)
- Q&A și consolidare (5 min)

### Module 2 — Identificarea taskurilor potrivite (60 min)
- Exercițiu: „Ce pot delega AI-ului?”
- Criterii de selecție: repetitive, structurate, cu text, cu feedback rapid
- Când NU să folosești AI

### Module 3 — Prompting ca abilitate (90 min)
- Structura unui prompt eficient
- Tehnici: rol, context, format, constrângeri, exemple, iterare
- Exerciții practice în perechi

### Module 4 — Aplicații practice pe roluri (90 min)
- Profesori: planuri de lecție, feedback, comunicate
- Manageri: rapoarte, emailuri, sinteze de întâlniri
- Specialiști: cercetare, documentație, prezentări
- Antreprenori: propuneri, oferte, strategii de comunicare

### Module 5 — Verificare și editare (60 min)
- Cum să citești critic un output AI
- Detectarea informațiilor false sau parțiale
- Adaptarea la vocea și valorile proprii

### Module 6 — Plan personal de utilizare (30 min)
- Crearea unui plan de 30 de zile
- Identificarea unui „buddy AI” în echipă
- Resurse de continuare

## 9. Assessment

### Formative assessment
- Observarea exercițiilor practice
- Feedback la prompturi
- Auto-evaluare la finalul fiecărui modul

### Summative assessment
- **Practical deliverable:** Each participant produces one AI-assisted work asset relevant to their role.
- **Reflection brief:** 150–250 words on what they will use AI for, what they will not use it for, and how they will maintain control.

### Practical deliverable rubric

| Criterion | Excellent (4) | Good (3) | Acceptable (2) | Needs revision (1) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Role relevance** | Asset directly supports the participant's real work | Asset is relevant to role | Asset is generic but usable | Asset is not relevant |
| **Prompt quality** | Prompt includes role, context, format and constraints | Prompt includes 3 of 4 elements | Prompt includes 1–2 elements | Prompt is vague |
| **Human editing** | Output is fully adapted to participant's voice and context | Minor edits needed | Noticeably generic | No personalisation |
| **Accuracy & safety** | Facts verified; no sensitive data used; ethical checklist followed | Minor verification issues | One significant risk | Unverified or unsafe use |
| **Reflection** | Clear 30-day plan with first step | Plan present but vague | Plan incomplete | No plan |

### Success indicators
- 100% of participants submit a practical deliverable.
- 90% of participants report increased confidence in using AI.
- 80% of participants identify at least 3 tasks they will delegate to AI within 30 days.

### 10.1 Troubleshooting guide

| Issue | Solution |
|---|---|
| AI tool is slow or unavailable | Switch to a backup tool (Gemini, Copilot, Claude). Use offline prompt-writing exercises. |
| Participant enters sensitive data | Stop immediately. Use it as a live learning moment. Refer to privacy checklist. |
| AI produces false information | Use it in Module 5. Ask the group to identify the error and correct it. |
| Participant is resistant or fearful | Acknowledge the concern. Use the „AI as calculator, not replacement" framing. |
| Participant wants advanced technical content | Redirect to the program scope. Offer resources for further technical learning. |

### 10.4 Ethical and privacy checklist

Before using any AI tool, I confirm that:
- [ ] I am not entering personal data of students, patients, clients or employees.
- [ ] I am not entering confidential, classified or commercially sensitive information.
- [ ] I know which AI tool I am using and whether inputs may be used to train the model.
- [ ] I will verify facts and not assume the output is correct.
- [ ] I will edit the output to reflect my own voice and professional judgement.
- [ ] I will take responsibility for the final result.
- [ ] I have checked my organisation's AI-use policy (if one exists).

## 11. Sector adaptations

### Education
- **Focus:** lesson planning, feedback to students, parent communication, administrative emails, differentiation of materials.
- **Privacy note:** never input student personal data into public AI tools.

#### Example: Education prompt
```
Rol: Ești un profesor de limba română pentru clasa a VIII-a.
Context: Elevii au terminat un eseu despre personajul principal din „Ion".
Task: Generează 5 feedbackuri scurte, constructive și personalizate pe care le pot adapta pentru elevii mei. Nu folosi nume reale.
Format: 5 paragrafe scurte.
Constrângere: Folosește un ton încurajator, nu critic.
```

#### Example deliverable: Lesson plan outline
AI generates a skeleton; teacher adds learning objectives, assessment questions and differentiation notes.

### Healthcare
- **Focus:** patient communication templates, summarising guidelines, public health materials, internal reporting.
- **Privacy note:** no patient data, no clinical decisions delegated to AI, all outputs reviewed by qualified staff.

#### Example: Healthcare prompt
```
Rol: Ești un asistent medical care comunică clar cu pacienții.
Context: Trebuie să explici unui pacient adult cum să se pregătească pentru o analiză de sânge de rutină.
Task: Scrie un text scurt, calm și clar pe care personalul medical îl poate adapta.
Format: 3 paragrafe.
Constrângere: Fără date personale, fără diagnostic, fără recomandări medicale specifice.
```

#### Example deliverable: Communication template
AI draft is reviewed and approved by qualified medical staff before use.

### Public administration
- **Focus:** official correspondence, reports, public communication, meeting summaries, translation support.
- **Compliance note:** follow institutional AI policy; no confidential or classified data in public tools.

#### Example: Public administration prompt
```
Rol: Ești un funcționar public care redactează comunicate oficiale.
Context: O primărie anunță deschiderea unui nou centru comunitar.
Task: Generează o primă variantă a comunicatului, cu ton neutru și accesibil.
Format: 200–250 cuvinte.
Constrângere: Fără jargon administrativ greoi.
```

#### Example deliverable: Public communication draft
Human edits ensure legal accuracy, institutional tone and factual correctness.

### Business / SMEs
- **Focus:** client proposals, marketing content, HR communications, strategy documents, competitor research.
- **Note:** develop internal AI-use guidelines and establish human-in-the-loop review.

#### Example: Business prompt
```
Rol: Ești un manager care trimite un email de follow-up după o întâlnire cu clientul.
Context: S-a discutat despre o colaborare de consultanță, dar nu s-au finalizat termenii.
Task: Scrie un email profesional, prietenos și clar care reia punctele cheie și propune un nou call.
Format: Email scurt (maxim 150 cuvinte).
Constrângere: Ton încrezător, fără presiune agresivă.
```

#### Example deliverable: Client email or proposal section
AI draft is reviewed for accuracy, brand voice and commercial sensitivity.

### NGOs
- **Focus:** grant applications, donor communication, social media content, volunteer coordination.
- **Note:** align AI use with organisational values and beneficiary confidentiality.

## 15. CPD Annex

### 15.1 Learning hours

| Format | Contact hours | Self-study / reflection | Total learning hours | CPD points (proposed) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Online (2 × 3h) | 6h | 1h | 7h | 7 |
| On-site (1 day) | 7h | 1h | 8h | 8 |

| Experience Edition | 10h | 2h | 12h | 12 |

**Basis:** 1 CPD point per learning hour, consistent with typical CPD Register conventions. Final accreditation to be confirmed with the accrediting body.

### 15.2 Facilitator requirements

**Essential:**
- Demonstrated experience in training adults in a professional context.
- Practical experience using generative AI tools in their own work.
- Ability to explain technical concepts in non-technical language.
- Familiarity with data privacy and AI ethics basics.

**Desirable:**
- Background in education, healthcare, public administration or business (to match sector adaptations).
- Experience delivering both online and in-person training.
- Completion of the AnimaMinds AI Fără Haos facilitator induction.

**Facilitator ratio:** 1 facilitator per 20 participants; 2 facilitators for groups over 20.

### 15.3 Evaluation methodology

**Pre-program (T0):**
- Confidence questionnaire (5 items, Likert 1–5)
- Self-assessment of current AI use

**During program:**
- Facilitator observation checklist
- Participant worksheet completion
- Module-end quick checks

**Post-program (T1, within 48h):**
- Repeat confidence questionnaire
- Practical deliverable submission
- Reflection brief
- Satisfaction survey (5 items)

**Follow-up (T2, 30 days):**
- Short online survey: tasks delegated, time saved, challenges encountered
- Optional submission of a second deliverable

### 15.4 Quality assurance process

1. **Content review cycle:** Program content is reviewed every 6 months or after any major AI tool update, whichever comes first.
2. **Facilitator debrief:** After each delivery, facilitators complete a 15-minute debrief form noting what worked, what did not and any participant questions they could not answer.
3. **Participant feedback analysis:** Feedback scores and comments are reviewed after every 3 deliveries to identify trends.
4. **Update log:** All changes to the PMD are logged with date, reason and approver.
5. **Sector validation:** Sector-specific examples are reviewed by at least one subject-matter expert or practitioner every 12 months.
6. **Data privacy audit:** Once per year, the privacy checklist and tool recommendations are reviewed to ensure compliance with current regulations and tool terms of service.
