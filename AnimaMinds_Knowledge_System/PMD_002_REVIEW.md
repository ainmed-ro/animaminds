# Internal Review: PMD_002_Conversatii_care_Conteaza

**Review date:** 2026-07-12  
**Reviewer:** Cascade  
**Status:** Draft — PMD_002 not modified; review only

---

## Executive summary

PMD_002_Conversatii_care_Conteaza is a strong first draft that follows the approved PMD_001_v2 template closely. It is well aligned with the COMUNICARE & COLABORARE category and successfully avoids deep conflict-management territory. The sector adaptations are present, and the tone is consistent with AnimaMinds.

Main areas for improvement: assessment specificity, facilitator guidance for emotional safety, online format adaptations, and a clearer distinction from Calm sub Presiune in the difficult-conversation module.

**Overall readiness:** 7.5 / 10 — good draft, minor refinements needed before approval.

---

## 1. Is the program promise clear?

**Verdict:** Yes.

The promise `Cuvintele potrivite schimbă totul.` is emotional and memorable. The five-point definition of "the right words" adds needed clarity. The distinction between connection and correction is strong.

**Suggested improvement:** Add one concrete example of a transformed conversation to make the promise tangible.

**Score:** 8 / 10

## 3. What can a participant do the next day after completing the program?

**Verdict:** Clear and practical.

A participant can:
- Prepare and deliver a structured feedback message.
- Reframe a harsh sentence into a connecting one.
- Use active listening in an important conversation.
- Apply the psychological safety checklist before a difficult discussion.
- Identify and prepare for 2–3 important conversations.

**Suggested improvement:** Add a "Next-day toolkit" list in Section 3 or a summary box.

**Score:** 8 / 10

## 5. Are there overlaps between modules?

**Verdict:** Minor overlap.

- **Module 1 and Module 4:** Both touch on difficult communication. Module 1 introduces the concept; Module 4 applies it. Boundary is acceptable but could be sharper.
- **Module 3 and Module 4:** Feedback is a subset of difficult conversations. The program correctly separates „feedback for change" from „difficult news / disagreement."
- **Module 5 and Module 4:** Both involve dialogue under tension. Module 5 focuses on alignment; Module 4 focuses on delivering hard messages.

**Suggested improvement:** Add a one-sentence facilitator note at the start of Modules 3, 4 and 5 clarifying the unique focus of each.

**Score:** 7 / 10

## 8. Which resources are missing?

**Missing resources identified:**

1. **Emotional safety protocol** — what to do if a participant becomes upset.
2. **Observer role cards** — for participants who prefer not to act.
3. **Sample videos or transcripts** of good vs. poor communication.
4. **Conflict escalation red flags** — brief guide on when to refer to PMD_004 / mediation.
5. **Evaluation rubric for listening** — currently only the feedback rubric is detailed.
6. **Parent/guardian communication addendum** for Education.
7. **Clinical escalation note** for Healthcare.
8. **Facilitator self-care note** — this content can be emotionally demanding.

**Score:** 7 / 10

---

## 9. Which sections need improvement before CPD submission?

**Critical gaps for CPD:**

1. **Emotional safety and safeguarding** — needs explicit protocol.
2. **Differentiation between this program and therapy/mediation** — important for scope protection.
3. **Facilitator emotional competence** — not just training skills, but capacity to hold space.
4. **Evaluation follow-up metric** — how many conversations were initiated and what changed.
5. **CPD annex is present but could be tightened** — align QA cycle with communication research updates.

**Suggested improvement:** Add a facilitator emotional-safety protocol and a scope boundary statement.

**Score:** 6 / 10

## Priority actions before approval

1. **Add emotional safety protocol** for facilitators (what to do if a participant becomes upset).
2. **Clarify LO6 evidence** — written reflection or planned repair script.
3. **Add observer role cards** and chat-based alternatives for online role-play.
4. **Add a listening-skills observation rubric** alongside the feedback rubric.
5. **Add scope boundary statement** distinguishing this program from therapy, mediation or conflict management.
6. **Add one deeper scenario per sector**.

---

## Conclusion

PMD_002 is a strong first draft that closely follows the approved master template and respects the boundary with Calm sub Presiune. It needs strengthening in emotional safety, facilitator guidance and online adaptations before it is approval-ready.
