## 1. Purpose

**Calm sub Presiune** helps professionals manage conflict, regulate their emotions and communicate with clarity and professionalism in high-stakes, tense or confrontational situations.

The program develops the inner stability and practical judgement needed to respond rather than react when pressure rises, disagreement deepens or situations become emotionally charged.

**Scope statement:**
This program is **not therapy, counselling or mediation**. It does not diagnose, treat or resolve deep interpersonal trauma, chronic conflict, mental health conditions or legal disputes. If such issues emerge, the facilitator refers participants to appropriate professional support and returns the group to the conflict-management and self-regulation skills focus of the program.

---

## 2. Challenge solved

| Problem | Consequence |
|---|---|
| Professionals feel overwhelmed in tense conversations | Reactive responses damage relationships and credibility |
| Conflicts escalate because they are avoided or mishandled | Small issues become major disputes |
| Emotional hijacking leads to regretful words or decisions | Trust breaks down, performance suffers |
| Teams lack a shared approach for de-escalation | Inconsistent responses, blame culture |
| High-pressure roles (medical, education, administration) create cumulative stress | Burnout, absenteeism, errors |

The program solves these by building emotional self-regulation, conflict-management and de-escalation skills in a practical, professional framework.

---

## 3. Program promise

> Pauză. Respirație. Reconstrucție.

**What the promise means in this program:**

Participants leave able to:
1. **Pause** before reacting in a high-pressure moment.
2. **Regulate** their own emotional state quickly.
3. **Read** the situation and choose a de-escalation strategy.
4. **Respond** with clarity, calm and professionalism.
5. **Recover** and rebuild the working relationship afterwards when appropriate.

Participants will leave the program able to:
- Recognise early signs of emotional escalation in themselves and others.
- Use grounding and regulation techniques in real time.
- De-escalate tense interactions without avoiding the issue.
- Set boundaries firmly and respectfully under pressure.
- Apply a structured conflict-management framework to professional situations.

## 5. Learning outcomes

By the end of the program, participants will be able to:

1. **Recognise** the physiological and behavioural signs of escalation in themselves and others.
2. **Apply** at least 3 emotional self-regulation techniques during a tense interaction.
3. **Analyse** a conflict situation using a structured framework (positions, interests, needs).
4. **Choose** an appropriate de-escalation strategy based on the level of intensity.
5. **Respond** with a calm, clear, professional message under pressure.
6. **Set and maintain** boundaries when confronted with aggression, blame or unreasonable demands.
7. **Create** a personal calm-under-pressure plan for the next 30 days.
8. **Identify** when a situation requires referral to HR, mediation, management or professional support.

### Learning outcomes ↔ assessment matrix

| LO | Assessment method | Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| LO1 Recognise escalation signs | Scenario observation + self-report checklist | Participant identifies at least 3 signs in a case study |
| LO2 Apply self-regulation techniques | In-session practice + reflection | Demonstrates one technique and describes when to use it |
| LO3 Analyse conflict using framework | Conflict mapping worksheet | Completed positions/interests/needs map |
| LO4 Choose de-escalation strategy | Scenario-based quiz + role-play | Selects and justifies strategy |
| LO5 Respond calmly under pressure | Role-play deliverable | Calm, clear, professional response observed |
| LO6 Set boundaries | Role-play or written script | Boundary stated respectfully and maintained |
| LO7 Create personal plan | 30-Day Calm Plan template | Completed plan with trigger, technique and first step |
| LO8 Identify referral needs | Ethical/safety scenario cards | Correctly identifies referral path |

## 7. Program structure

### Module 1 — Ce se întâmplă sub presiune (60 min)
**Practical hook (first 20 min):** Participants watch or read a short high-pressure scenario and identify the exact moment when the professional lost control. They then practise a 60-second grounding technique together.

- Ciclul presiune-reacție: stimul, interpretare, emoție, reacție (15 min)
- Semne de escaladare în corp, voce, cuvinte, comportament (15 min)
- De la reacție la răspuns: diferența dintre a reacționa și a alege (10 min)

### Module 2 — Autoreglarea emoțională (60 min)
- Tehnici de reglare: respirație, ancorare, distanțare, etichetare emoțională
- Exerciții practice: fiecare participant testează 3 tehnici
- Alegerea unei tehnici „de bază" pentru situații profesionale

### Module 3 — Anatomia unui conflict (60 min)
- Poziții, interese, nevoi (PIN framework)
- Nivele de intensitate a conflictului: disconfort, dispută, dezacord, ostilitate
- Când este conflictul productiv și când devine distructiv

### Module 4 — Strategii de de-escaladare (90 min)
- Tehnici verbale: redirecționare, reformulare, validarea parțială, limite clare
- Tehnici non-verbale: ton, ritm, proximitate, pauză
- „Cuvintele de sub presiune": formulări care dezamorsează
- Exercițiu: transformarea unei replici reactive într-una calmă

**Boundary with PMD_002:** This module does **not** teach active listening, feedback or communication mechanics. Those skills are assumed as prerequisites or as tools used by PMD_004 participants in calmer moments. PMD_004 focuses on what to do when the situation is already heated.

### Module 5 — Limite, siguranță și referire (60 min)
- Cum să spui „stop" profesional
- Când și cum să ieși dintr-o interacțiune escaladată
- Când să ceri ajutor: manager, HR, securitate, mediere, consiliere
- Protecția personală și a instituției

### Module 6 — Plan personal de calm sub presiune (30 min)
- Identificarea triggerelor personale
- Alegerea a 2–3 tehnici de reglare
- Definirea primului pas pentru următoarele 30 de zile
- Resurse de continuare

## 8. Activities

| Activity | Purpose | Materials |
|---|---|---|
| Escalation Spotting | Identify early signs of pressure in self and others | Video/audio scenario cards |
| Regulation Lab | Test and select personal regulation techniques | Technique cards |
| PIN Mapping | Analyse conflict through positions, interests, needs | Conflict mapping worksheet |
| De-escalation Rewrite | Transform reactive responses into calm professional responses | Rewrite worksheet |
| Pressure Role-Play | Practise staying calm while receiving pressure | Scenario cards |
| Boundary Script | Write and deliver a firm, respectful boundary | Script template |
| 30-Day Calm Plan | Personal commitment and preparation | Plan template |

### Online adaptations
- Escalation Spotting uses shared screen and chat responses.
- Regulation Lab uses guided audio/video with cameras off.
- PIN Mapping uses shared digital whiteboard.
- Role-plays use breakout rooms with observer roles.
- Boundary Script can be done in writing or via chat.

### Observer roles for online and on-site role-play

Each role-play group of three includes:
- **Professional** — manages the pressure situation.
- **Other party** — presents the pressure or conflict.
- **Observer** — notes signs of escalation, regulation techniques and de-escalation choices using the Calm Response Observation Guide.

Observer swaps role each round.

### Experience Edition adaptations
- Add a reflective evening session on personal triggers.
- Include body-based regulation exercises in nature or movement.
- Use longer, immersive scenarios with debrief circles.

## 9. Assessment

### Formative assessment
- Observation of regulation technique practice
- Peer feedback on de-escalation rewrites
- Scenario-based discussions

### Summative assessment
- **Practical deliverable:** One rewritten high-pressure response + one boundary script.
- **Reflection brief:** 150–250 words on a personal trigger, the regulation technique chosen and how it will be applied.

### Practical deliverable rubric — Calm response under pressure

| Criterion | Excellent (4) | Good (3) | Acceptable (2) | Needs revision (1) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Self-regulation signal** | Response shows clear pause or grounding before action | Brief pause implied | No visible regulation | Escalatory reaction |
| **Clarity** | Message is calm, specific and unambiguous | Mostly clear | Vague or emotional | Confusing or hostile |
| **De-escalation** | Choice of strategy fits the situation and reduces intensity | Reduces intensity | Neutral effect | Escalates or avoids |
| **Boundaries** | Sets or maintains limits respectfully | Limits present but soft | Limits unclear | Aggressive or absent |
| **Referral awareness** | Knows when to involve others | Mentions possible referral | Unclear | Ignores need for support |

### Success indicators
- 100% of participants submit a rewritten response and boundary script.
- 90% of participants report increased confidence in handling pressure.
- 80% of participants can name at least 2 self-regulation techniques they will use within 30 days.

### 10.1 Troubleshooting guide

| Issue | Solution |
|---|---|
| Participant becomes overwhelmed by a scenario | Pause, offer observer role or break, check in privately. |
| Group minimises conflict as „not serious" | Use real cases from the sector, anonymised, with permission. |
| Participant wants techniques to „control" others | Reframe: we regulate ourselves and influence the dynamic, not control others. |
| Existing team conflict surfaces | Stay in program scope: individual skills and team protocols, not mediation. |
| Scenario feels too role-specific for mixed cohort | Use generic pressure scenarios plus sector breakout options. |

### 10.2 Follow-up email sequence

**Day 2 — First trigger:**
> Subiect: Primul tău moment sub presiune din planul de 30 de zile
> Conținut: Ce trigger ai observat? Ce tehnică de reglare ai folosit? Răspunde scurt.

**Day 7 — Reflection:**
> Subiect: Ce a funcționat diferit când ai simțit presiunea?
> Conținut: Reflecție rapidă: ai reușit să faci pauză? Ce ai spus diferit? Ce ai învățat?

**Day 14 — Resource:**
> Subiect: 5 fraze care dezamorsează o situație tensionată
> Conținut: Listă cu formulări profesionale pentru situații dificile.

**Day 30 — Outcome:**
> Subiect: Planul tău de 30 de zile — rezultate
> Conținut: Survey scurt: câte situații sub presiune ai gestionat diferit? Ce impact ai observat?

### 10.4 Pre-response checklist

Before responding in a high-pressure moment, I confirm that:
- [ ] I have taken at least one breath.
- [ ] I have named my emotion internally (e.g., „I feel frustrated").
- [ ] I have separated the person from the behaviour.
- [ ] I have chosen one clear message, not three.
- [ ] I have checked whether the setting is safe and appropriate.
- [ ] I am prepared to set a boundary or pause if needed.
- [ ] I know who to involve if the situation escalates further.

### 10.5 Facilitator emotional safety protocol

**Purpose:** Protect participants and maintain the program scope when emotionally charged or conflict-related content emerges.

**If a participant becomes visibly upset:**
1. Pause the activity calmly.
2. Acknowledge the emotion: *„Văd că acest scenariu atinge ceva personal. Nu trebuie să continuăm."*
3. Offer options: observe, take a break, or speak privately.
4. Do not offer counselling, diagnosis or advice beyond the program scope.
5. Refer to appropriate support if needed (HR, EAP, therapist, manager).
6. Return the group to the skills focus.

**If a participant discloses trauma, abuse, severe conflict or mental health crisis:**
1. Thank them for trusting the group.
2. State clearly: *„Acest program nu oferă terapie, consiliere sau mediere. Vă recomand să discutați cu un specialist."*
3. Offer a private follow-up to identify referral options.
4. Document the referral offer.

**Facilitator self-care:**
- Debrief after emotionally intense sessions.
- Avoid taking responsibility for resolving participants' conflicts.
- Take breaks between consecutive deliveries.

## 11. Sector adaptations

### Education
- **Focus:** conflict between students, tense parent-teacher meetings, classroom disruption, colleague disagreement under exam pressure.
- **Note:** teacher must model calm under pressure; scenarios should respect child safeguarding.

#### Example scenario: Education
A parent becomes increasingly angry during a meeting about their child's grade, raising their voice and accusing the teacher of unfairness.

**Calm response focus:** pause, ground, validate the parent's concern without agreeing with the accusation, restate facts, propose a clear next step, set boundary if aggression continues.

### Healthcare
- **Focus:** distressed patients or family members, tense handovers, disagreement over treatment, blame under time pressure.
- **Note:** patient safety and dignity are paramount; de-escalation never replaces clinical judgement.

#### Example scenario: Healthcare
A patient's family member confronts a nurse in the corridor, demanding immediate answers and becoming physically close and loud.

**Calm response focus:** ground, lower voice, acknowledge distress, set physical and conversational boundary, explain next step, involve senior staff if needed.

### Public Administration
- **Focus:** angry citizens at counters, complaints about delays, public meetings with hostile audiences, inter-departmental tension.
- **Compliance note:** de-escalation must remain neutral, respectful and within institutional authority.

#### Example scenario: Public Administration
A citizen at the counter becomes verbally aggressive because a document was not processed on time, despite not having submitted the correct papers.

**Calm response focus:** regulate, listen to the emotion, restate the procedure calmly, avoid blame, offer a concrete next step, call security/manager if threats appear.

### Business / SMEs
- **Focus:** high-pressure client calls, conflict between departments, blame in project failures, aggressive negotiation.
- **Note:** calm under pressure protects relationships and commercial outcomes.

#### Example scenario: Business
A client calls shouting about a missed delivery deadline, threatening to cancel the contract.

**Calm response focus:** pause, acknowledge impact, avoid defensive explanations, present facts and recovery plan, set boundary if language becomes abusive.

### NGOs
- **Focus:** distressed beneficiaries, tense partner negotiations, activist burnout, donor pressure.
- **Note:** values-based boundaries protect staff and mission.

#### Example scenario: NGO
A volunteer becomes emotionally overwhelmed during a beneficiary visit and confronts the coordinator about insufficient support.

**Calm response focus:** validate the volunteer's experience, regulate the conversation, separate the immediate emotion from the operational issue, agree a concrete follow-up.

### 15.2 Facilitator requirements

**Essential:**
- Demonstrated experience in training adults in stress management, conflict handling or resilience.
- Strong facilitation skills for emotionally loaded topics.
- Ability to maintain boundaries and keep conversations within the program scope.
- Familiarity with self-regulation techniques and basic conflict frameworks.
- Emotional maturity and capacity to hold space for discomfort without rescuing or advising.

**Desirable:**
- Background in education, healthcare, public administration, business or NGOs.
- Experience delivering both online and in-person experiential learning.
- Completion of the AnimaMinds Calm sub Presiune facilitator induction.

**Facilitator ratio:** 1 facilitator per 14 participants; 2 facilitators for groups over 14 or where emotional intensity is expected.

### 15.3 Evaluation methodology

**Pre-program (T0):**
- Stress reactivity questionnaire (5 items, Likert 1–5)
- Self-assessment of current conflict-management habits

**During program:**
- Facilitator observation checklist
- Peer feedback on de-escalation rewrites and boundary scripts
- Module-end quick checks

**Post-program (T1, within 48h):**
- Repeat stress reactivity questionnaire
- Rewritten response + boundary script submission
- Reflection brief
- Satisfaction survey (5 items)

**Follow-up (T2, 30 days):**
- Short online survey: pressure situations handled, techniques used, support sought
- Optional second reflection brief

### 15.4 Quality assurance process

1. **Content review cycle:** Program content is reviewed every 6 months or after significant research updates on stress, conflict or de-escalation.
2. **Facilitator debrief:** After each delivery, facilitators complete a 15-minute debrief form noting what worked, what did not and any emotional intensity.
3. **Participant feedback analysis:** Feedback scores and comments are reviewed after every 3 deliveries.
4. **Update log:** All changes to the PMD are logged with date, reason and approver.
5. **Sector validation:** Sector-specific scenarios are reviewed by at least one practitioner every 12 months.
6. **Safety audit:** Once per year, scenarios and role-play boundaries are reviewed to ensure they do not trigger unresolved trauma or require therapeutic intervention.
