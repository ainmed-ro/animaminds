## 5. Facilitator qualifications

### Essential
- Demonstrated experience in training adults in resilience, conflict management or stress management.
- Strong facilitation skills for emotionally sensitive topics.
- Ability to maintain program scope and refer beyond-scope issues appropriately.
- Familiarity with self-regulation and de-escalation frameworks.

### Desirable
- Background in education, healthcare, public administration, business or NGOs.
- Experience delivering both online and in-person experiential learning.
- Completion of the AnimaMinds Calm sub Presiune facilitator induction.

### Facilitator ratio
- 1 facilitator per 16 participants.
- 2 facilitators for groups over 16 or where high-pressure cases are expected.

**Rationale:** Pressure and conflict scenarios carry higher emotional intensity and require close observation, pauses and potential referral. The 1:16 ratio keeps participant safety manageable.

## 6. Assessment and evaluation methodology

### 6.1 Completion criteria

Participants must:
- Attend at least 80% of contact hours.
- Submit one rewritten high-pressure response + one boundary script within 48 hours.
- Submit a reflection brief (150–250 words) within 48 hours.
- Complete the post-program satisfaction survey.

Remediation assignment available within 14 days if criteria not met.

### 6.2 Pre-program (T0)

**Confidence questionnaire (5 items, Likert 1–5)**
1. I can recognise early signs of escalation in myself.
2. I can regulate my emotions in tense moments.
3. I can de-escalate tense interactions.
4. I can set boundaries under pressure.
5. I know when to refer a situation to HR or specialist support.

### 6.3 During program

- Facilitator observation checklist
- Peer feedback on de-escalation rewrites
- Module-end quick checks

### 6.4 Post-program (T1)

- Repeat confidence questionnaire
- Practical deliverable submission
- Reflection brief
- Satisfaction survey (5 items)

### 6.5 Follow-up (T2, 30 days)

- Short online survey: pressure situations managed, techniques used, referrals made
- Optional second reflection brief

## 7. Assessment rubric

| Criterion | Excellent (4) | Good (3) | Acceptable (2) | Needs revision (1) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Self-regulation signal | Clear pause or grounding | Brief pause implied | No visible regulation | Escalatory reaction |
| Clarity | Calm, specific and unambiguous | Mostly clear | Vague or emotional | Confusing or hostile |
| De-escalation | Strategy fits and reduces intensity | Reduces intensity | Neutral effect | Escalates or avoids |
| Boundaries | Sets limits respectfully | Limits present but soft | Limits unclear | Aggressive or absent |
| Referral awareness | Knows when to involve others | Mentions possible referral | Unclear | Ignores need for support |
