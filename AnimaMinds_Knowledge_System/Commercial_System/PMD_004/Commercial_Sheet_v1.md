## The problem we solve

| Challenge | Cost of inaction |
|---|---|
| Too many priorities, no clear direction | Effort is scattered, results are mediocre |
| Decisions are reactive or delayed | Opportunities missed, problems grow |
| Teams are misaligned on what matters most | Conflict, duplicated work, low engagement |
| Change feels overwhelming or imposed | Resistance, fatigue, failed initiatives |
| Leaders lack a shared language for direction and trade-offs | Inconsistent messaging, confusion |

---

## What participants will be able to do

1. Explain the difference between a problem, a decision and a direction.
2. Apply at least 3 prioritisation or decision-making frameworks.
3. Identify key stakeholders and criteria for a significant decision.
4. Analyse a change situation using a recognised change model.
5. Map common sources of resistance and choose appropriate responses.
6. Communicate direction clearly to a team or group.
7. Create a 30-day action plan with priorities, owners and first steps.
8. Evaluate when to decide quickly, when to involve others and when to delay.
