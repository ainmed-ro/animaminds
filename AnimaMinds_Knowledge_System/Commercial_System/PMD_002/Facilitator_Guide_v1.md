## 1. Facilitator role

You are not a therapist or mediator. You are a communication skills facilitator who helps participants:
- Prepare and deliver feedback with clarity and respect.
- Listen actively, especially in high-stakes conversations.
- Express disagreement without damaging relationships.
- Align teams through dialogue.

Your job is to keep the group in the communication-skills lane and to refer deeper issues to appropriate professional support.

## 5. Module scripts

### Module 1 — Comunicarea care construiește (60 min)

**Objective:** By the end of this module, participants can distinguish feedback types and understand psychological safety basics.

**Slides 2–6**

**Hook (20 min):**
- 0–5 min: Ask participants to recall a recent conversation that went badly.
- 5–15 min: Identify the one sentence that changed everything.
- 15–20 min: Rewrite that sentence using the connect-before-correct technique.

**Discussion questions:**
- What made the original sentence harmful?
- What does the rewritten version preserve or change?
- How did the other person likely feel?

**Content walkthrough (30 min):**
- Slides 7–10: From monologue to dialogue.
- Slides 11–15: Types of feedback — appreciation, evaluation, coaching.
- Slides 16–20: Barriers to communication and psychological safety introduction.

**Time check at 40 min:** Hook and content must be complete. If behind, shorten barrier discussion.

**Consolidation (10 min):**
- Participants complete Workbook Section 2.

**Deliverable:** Workbook Section 3 (Sentence Rewrite) completed.

---

### Module 2 — Ascultarea activă (60 min)

**Objective:** By the end of this module, participants can paraphrase, clarify, summarise and validate in conversation.

**Slides 21–25**

**Mini-lesson (15 min):**
- Slides 21–23: Three levels of listening — internal, focused, global.
- Slides 24–25: Four techniques — paraphrase, clarification, summarising, emotional validation.

**Activity: Listening Triangles (40 min)**
- 0–5 min: Form groups of 3.
- 5–15 min: Round 1 — Speaker, Listener, Observer. Listener practises paraphrase.
- 15–25 min: Round 2 — Roles rotate. Listener practises clarification.
- 25–35 min: Round 3 — Roles rotate. Listener practises summarising + validation.
- 35–40 min: Debrief in small groups.

**Time check at 30 min:** Round 2 should be underway.

**Debrief (5 min):**
- What was hardest: listening without interrupting or listening without fixing?
- What changed when the listener validated the emotion?

**Deliverable:** Workbook Section 4 completed.

---

### Module 3 — Feedback care produce schimbare (90 min)

**Objective:** By the end of this module, participants can prepare a structured feedback message.

**Slides 26–35**

**Mini-lesson (20 min):**
- Slides 26–30: Feedback structure — situation, behaviour, impact, request.
- Slides 31–35: Positive feedback and improvement feedback; common mistakes.

**Activity: Feedback Script (60 min)**
- 0–10 min: Participants choose a real or realistic feedback situation.
- 10–30 min: Write first draft using the 4-sentence structure.
- 30–45 min: Pair share. Partner checks for clarity, connection and actionability.
- 45–60 min: Revise and prepare to share one sentence with the group.

**Time check at 45 min:** First draft should be complete.

**Debrief (10 min):**
- What made a message feel safe vs. threatening?
- How did the request change the conversation?

**Deliverable:** Workbook Section 5 completed.

---

### Module 4 — Conversații dificile (90 min)

**Objective:** By the end of this module, participants can deliver difficult news or disagreement using the "I observe... I feel/think... I need..." structure.

**Slides 36–45**

**Mini-lesson (20 min):**
- Slides 36–40: How to say no, deliver bad news, manage emotions.
- Slides 41–45: The "I observe... I feel/think... I need..." technique.

**Activity: Difficult Conversation Role-Play (60 min)**
- 0–5 min: Introduce observer roles and Scenario Cards (Appendix A).
- 5–10 min: Groups of 3 assign Speaker, Recipient, Observer.
- 10–35 min: First role-play.
- 35–45 min: Debrief using Feedback Rubric.
- 45–60 min: Second role-play with swapped roles.

**Time check at 35 min:** First role-play must be complete.

**Debrief (10 min):**
- What felt easier in the second round?
- What would you do differently in a real conversation?

**Boundary reminder:** This is communication mechanics, not conflict mediation or therapy. Refer to PMD_003 / Calm sub Presiune or professional support if needed.

**Deliverable:** Workbook Section 6 completed.

---

### Module 5 — Alinierea și decizia în echipă (60 min)

**Objective:** By the end of this module, participants can use questions and listening to align a team around a decision.

**Slides 46–52**

**Mini-lesson (15 min):**
- Slides 46–48: Alignment vs. persuasion.
- Slides 49–52: Powerful questions and consensus minus one.

**Activity: Team Alignment Map (35 min)**
- 0–5 min: Present a realistic team decision scenario.
- 5–15 min: Map initial positions.
- 15–30 min: Use powerful questions to find shared criteria and decision.
- 30–35 min: Record decision and first steps.

**Debrief (10 min):**
- What question unlocked the alignment?
- What is the risk of deciding by monologue?

**Deliverable:** Workbook Section 8 completed.

---

### Module 6 — Plan personal de comunicare (30 min)

**Objective:** By the end of this module, participants have a concrete 30-day plan.

**Slides 53–55**

**Activity (20 min):**
- 0–5 min: Introduce Workbook Section 10.
- 5–15 min: Participants complete the 30-Day Conversation Plan.
- 15–20 min: Pair share of first conversation and first step.

**Closing (10 min):**
- Each participant shares one commitment in one sentence.
- Remind them of the follow-up email sequence.
- End with the promise: *„Cuvintele potrivite schimbă totul."*

**Deliverable:** Workbook Section 10 completed.

## 6. Closing script

**Time:** 5 minutes  
**Slide 56:** Closing slide

> Am ajuns la final. În următoarele 30 de zile veți primi patru emailuri care vă vor ajuta să rămâneți în practică.
>
> Înainte să plecăm, am trei întrebări:
> 1. Care este prima conversație pe care o veți pregăti în această săptămână?
> 2. Ce tehnică veți folosi pentru a crea siguranță psihologică?
> 3. Cui veți cere feedback după acea conversație?
>
> Amintiți-vă: comunicarea bună nu este despre a avea dreptate. Este despre a clarifica, a conecta și a avansa împreună.
>
> Cuvintele potrivite schimbă totul. Mulțumesc.

## 7. Assessment guide

### 7.1 Practical deliverable

Each participant submits one completed feedback script + one 4-sentence message ready to send.

Use the PMD rubric:

| Criterion | Excellent (4) | Good (3) | Acceptable (2) | Needs revision (1) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Clarity | Message is unambiguous and specific | Minor ambiguity | Vague or general | Confusing |
| Connection | Tone builds trust and openness | Mostly respectful | Neutral or slightly distant | Hostile or cold |
| Actionability | Clear request or next step | Next step implied | No clear next step | No request |
| Psychological safety | Recipient's perspective acknowledged | Some acknowledgment | Little acknowledgment | Dismissive |
| Reflection | Clear insight and planned change | Insight present | Surface reflection | No reflection |

### 7.2 Active Listening Rubric

Use during Listening Triangles and role-play observation.

| Criterion | Excellent (4) | Good (3) | Acceptable (2) | Needs revision (1) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Paraphrase | Captures content and feeling accurately | Captures content accurately | Partial capture | Misrepresents speaker |
| Open questions | Expands understanding | Mostly open questions | Mix of open/closed | Dominates with statements |
| Validation | Acknowledges emotions without agreeing or fixing | Acknowledges briefly | Minimal acknowledgment | Ignores or dismisses emotions |
| Summarising | Pulls together themes and checks accuracy | Summarises main points | Partial summary | No summarising |
| Non-verbal presence | Eye contact, posture and silence support listening | Mostly present | Distracted at times | Distracted or interrupting |

### 7.3 Success indicators
- 100% of participants submit a feedback script.
- 90% of participants report increased confidence in giving feedback.
- 80% of participants identify at least 2 important conversations they will initiate within 30 days.

## 10. Troubleshooting

| Issue | Response |
|---|---|
| Participant is uncomfortable with role-play | Offer written or observer role. Validate discomfort. |
| Group has existing conflict | Stay within scope: communication mechanics. Refer to PMD_003 if deep conflict. |
| Participant dominates discussions | Use structured turn-taking and breakout rooms. |
| Emotional content surfaces | See Emotional Safety Protocol. |
| Role-play feels artificial | Use participants' own anonymised cases with permission. |
| Online participant declines video | Use chat-based role-play alternative. |

## Appendix A — Scenario Cards

### Card 1: Educație
**Situație:** Trebuie să vorbești cu un părinte despre faptul că fiul/fiica lui are dificultăți la matematică.
**Rol:** Profesorul vorbește; părintele primește.
**Focus:** Situație, comportament, impact, cerere.

### Card 2: Sănătate
**Situație:** Un coleg ajunge constant târziu la raport.
**Rol:** Asistentul senior dă feedback; asistentul întârziat primește.
**Focus:** Siguranța pacientului, respect, soluție comună.

### Card 3: Administrație publică
**Situație:** Trebuie să anunți echipa că un proiect popular este amânat din motive bugetare.
**Rol:** Șeful de departament; restul echipei.
**Focus:** Vestă proastă, aliniere, transparență.

### Card 4: Business
**Situație:** Într-o ședință, trebuie să contrazici politicos propunerea unui client.
**Rol:** Managerul; clientul.
**Focus:** Dezacord respectuos, alternative.

### Card 5: ONG
**Situație:** Un voluntar nu respectă procedura de raportare a incidentelor.
**Rol:** Coordonatorul; voluntarul.
**Focus:** Misiune, responsabilitate, reparație.

### Card 6: Coleg de echipă
**Situație:** Un coleg întrerupe frecvent în ședințe.
**Rol:** Colegul observator; colegul care întrerupe.
**Focus:** Feedback de la egal la egal.
