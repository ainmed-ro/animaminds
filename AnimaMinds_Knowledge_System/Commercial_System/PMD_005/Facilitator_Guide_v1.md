## 1. Facilitator role

You are a thinking-skills facilitator who helps participants:
- Question assumptions and information.
- Adapt when plans change.
- Generate creative solutions under constraints.
- Exercise sound judgement.
- Understand the complementary roles of human insight and AI.

Your job is to challenge assumptions respectfully and keep the program in the thinking-judgement lane, not AI tools, communication, conflict or strategy consulting.

## 5. Module scripts

### Module 1 — Gândirea în era AI (60 min)

**Objective:** Participants can identify what AI recommendations miss.

**Slides 2–6**

**Hook (20 min):**
- Present a short AI-generated recommendation.
- Ask: what is missing? (context, values, trade-offs, uncertainty)
- Discuss what a human must add.

**Content (30 min):**
- Slides 7–12: What AI can and cannot do.
- Slides 13–18: From information to meaning.
- Slides 19–20: Critical thinking as a professional habit.

**Time check at 40 min:** Hook and content complete.

**Deliverable:** Workbook Section 2.

---

### Module 2 — Unelte de gândire critică (90 min)

**Objective:** Participants can deconstruct arguments and identify assumptions.

**Slides 21–32**

**Mini-lesson (25 min):**
- Slides 21–26: Argument anatomy — claim, evidence, reasoning, assumptions.
- Slides 27–32: Tools — 5 Whys, cause analysis, devil's advocate.

**Activity: Argument Anatomy (60 min)**
- 0–10 min: Present a proposal from participants' sector.
- 10–30 min: Complete Argument Anatomy template.
- 30–45 min: Pair share.
- 45–60 min: Whole-group debrief.

**Time check at 45 min:** Templates mostly complete.

**Deliverable:** Workbook Section 3.

---

### Module 3 — Biasuri și judecata (60 min)

**Objective:** Participants can recognise biases and mitigate them.

**Slides 33–40**

**Mini-lesson (15 min):**
- Slides 33–36: Common biases.
- Slides 37–40: Mitigation structures.

**Activity: Bias Mitigation Workshop (40 min)**
- 0–5 min: Present sector-specific biased scenario.
- 5–25 min: In pairs, identify at least 3 biases and complete checklist.
- 25–40 min: Debrief mitigation actions.

**Deliverable:** Workbook Section 4.

---

### Module 4 — Creativitatea sub constrângere (60 min)

**Objective:** Participants can generate novel ideas within constraints.

**Slides 41–48**

**Mini-lesson (15 min):**
- Slides 41–44: From brainstorming to convergent thinking.
- Slides 45–48: SCAMPER and "What if" prompts.

**Activity: Constraint Creativity (40 min)**
- 0–5 min: Introduce a real problem with tight constraints.
- 5–25 min: Generate ideas using SCAMPER.
- 25–40 min: Select and refine one idea.

**Deliverable:** Workbook Section 5.

---

### Module 5 — Adaptabilitatea și învățarea (60 min)

**Objective:** Participants can revise plans when new information arrives.

**Slides 49–56**

**Mini-lesson (15 min):**
- Slides 49–52: OODA cycle.
- Slides 53–56: Stable in values, flexible in method.

**Activity: Adaptability Simulation (40 min)**
- 0–5 min: Teams receive a plan.
- 5–20 min: New information arrives; revise plan.
- 20–35 min: Second wave of new information; revise again.
- 35–40 min: Present revised plan and rationale.

**Deliverable:** Workbook Section 6.

---

### Module 6 — Plan personal (30 min)

**Objective:** Participants create a 30-day development plan.

**Slides 57–60**

**Activity (20 min):**
- Complete Workbook Section 9.
- Pair share focus area and first step.

**Closing (10 min):**
- Each participant states one commitment.
- Remind of follow-up emails.
- End with promise: *„Ceea ce te face om rămâne de neînlocuit."*

## 7. Assessment guide

### 7.1 Practical deliverable

Each participant submits Argument Anatomy + Bias Mitigation Checklist + revised Adaptability Plan + judgement case evaluation.

Use the PMD rubric:

| Criterion | Excellent (4) | Good (3) | Acceptable (2) | Needs revision (1) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Critical analysis | Clearly identifies assumptions, evidence and reasoning | Mostly clear | Some elements identified | Superficial or missing |
| Bias awareness | Recognises and mitigates relevant biases | Recognises biases | Limited awareness | No awareness |
| Creativity | Produces novel, feasible ideas within constraints | Mostly feasible | Few or obvious ideas | No relevant ideas |
| Adaptability | Revises approach thoughtfully when information changes | Revises with some reason | Minor revision | Rigid |
| Judgement quality | Decision is well-reasoned and criteria-based | Mostly reasoned | Weak justification | Opinion only |
| Reflection | Clear personal insight and commitment to practice | Insight present | Surface | Absent |

### 7.2 Success indicators
- 100% submit all four deliverables.
- 90% report increased awareness of their own thinking habits.
- 80% identify at least one area where they will rely more on human judgement.

## Appendix A — Sector Case Studies

### Education
**Case:** A teacher receives a highly recommended AI-generated lesson plan. It is efficient but does not fit the specific needs of her class. She must decide what to keep, adapt or reject.
**Thinking focus:** Identify assumptions in the AI output, apply criteria for student learning, adapt creatively, justify the final plan.

### Healthcare
**Case:** A medical team receives conflicting test results and patient history. They must decide whether to follow standard protocol or pursue a less common diagnosis.
**Thinking focus:** Weigh evidence, recognise availability bias, consider alternatives, explain judgement clearly to the team and patient.

### Public Administration
**Case:** An official must evaluate a popular but unproven policy proposal that is gaining media traction. The evidence is thin but political pressure is high.
**Thinking focus:** Separate evidence from rhetoric, identify assumptions, consider unintended consequences, make a defensible recommendation.

### Business
**Case:** A team is presented with a market research report that confirms what the leadership already believes. The team must decide whether to trust it or challenge it.
**Thinking focus:** Identify confirmation bias, test assumptions, seek disconfirming evidence, propose alternative interpretations.

### NGO
**Case:** An NGO is offered a large grant that would require shifting part of its mission toward a more fundable but less aligned activity.
**Thinking focus:** Test alignment with mission and values, evaluate long-term impact, consider stakeholder trust, defend the decision with clear reasoning.
