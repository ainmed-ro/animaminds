## The problem we solve

| Challenge | Cost of inaction |
|---|---|
| Professionals rely on templates or algorithms without questioning them | Poor decisions, missed risks, blind spots |
| People struggle to adapt when plans change | Stress, rigidity, lost opportunities |
| Teams generate many ideas but few good ones | Superficial innovation, wasted effort |
| Judgement is confused with opinion or gut feeling | Inconsistent decisions, weak justification |
| Fear of AI displacement reduces confidence and initiative | Disengagement, underinvestment in human skills |

---

## What participants will be able to do

1. Identify assumptions, evidence and reasoning in a given argument.
2. Apply at least 3 critical thinking tools to evaluate a real-world claim.
3. Recognise common cognitive biases and explain how they affect judgement.
4. Generate multiple creative solutions to a constrained problem.
5. Adapt a plan or approach in response to new, unexpected information.
6. Evaluate the quality of a decision using criteria beyond immediate outcome.
7. Create a personal development plan for strengthening human judgement.
8. Explain the complementary roles of human judgement and AI.

## What participants can do the next day

- Deconstruct an argument into claims, evidence and assumptions.
- Spot at least one cognitive bias in a real situation.
- Use SCAMPER or a constraint prompt to generate ideas.
- Pause before accepting an AI-generated recommendation.
- Start a 30-day thinking-habit plan.
