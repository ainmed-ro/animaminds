## 1. Purpose

**Busola Deciziilor** helps individuals, teams and organisations find clarity, set direction and make better decisions in contexts of uncertainty, change or competing priorities.

The program combines leadership thinking, prioritisation tools and change-management frameworks into a practical experience that produces a clear action plan and a shared sense of direction.

**Scope statement:**
This program is **not executive coaching, strategy consulting or psychotherapy**. It does not produce formal strategic plans, diagnose organisational dysfunction or treat individual mental health. It provides structured tools and reflective exercises that participants adapt to their own context.

---

## 2. Challenge solved

| Problem | Consequence |
|---|---|
| Too many priorities, no clear direction | Effort is scattered, results are mediocre |
| Decisions are reactive or delayed | Opportunities missed, problems grow |
| Teams are misaligned on what matters most | Conflict, duplicated work, low engagement |
| Change feels overwhelming or imposed | Resistance, fatigue, failed initiatives |
| Leaders lack a shared language for direction and trade-offs | Inconsistent messaging, confusion |

The program solves these by offering practical leadership and decision-making tools that create clarity and align people around action.

---

## 3. Program promise

> Claritate și direcție atunci când lucrurile par neclare.

**What the promise means in this program:**

Participants leave able to:
1. **Name** the real decision they need to make or the direction they need to set.
2. **Map** options, trade-offs and stakeholders clearly.
3. **Choose** a prioritisation or decision framework appropriate to the situation.
4. **Align** others around the decision through clarity and involvement.
5. **Act** with a concrete 30-day plan.

**Example:** A department head says *„Avem prea multe de făcut și nu știm cu ce să începem."* After the program she says *„Am clarificat 3 priorități strategice, am mapat stakeholderii și am un mesaj pe care îl comunic luni echipei."*

Participants will leave the program able to:
- Use at least 3 decision-making and prioritisation frameworks.
- Distinguish between urgent, important and strategic priorities.
- Identify and address common sources of resistance to change.
- Communicate direction in a way that creates alignment.
- Build a personal or team action plan for the next 30 days.

## 5. Learning outcomes

By the end of the program, participants will be able to:

1. **Explain** the difference between a problem, a decision and a direction.
2. **Apply** at least 3 prioritisation or decision-making frameworks to a real situation.
3. **Identify** the key stakeholders and criteria for a significant decision.
4. **Analyse** a change situation using a recognised change model.
5. **Map** common sources of resistance to change and choose appropriate responses.
6. **Communicate** direction clearly to a team or group.
7. **Create** a 30-day action plan with priorities, owners and first steps.
8. **Evaluate** when to decide quickly, when to involve others and when to delay.

## 7. Program structure

### Module 1 — Ce înseamnă claritatea (60 min)
**Practical hook (first 20 min):** Participants write down one decision or direction they have been avoiding. They share in pairs what makes it unclear. The facilitator introduces the idea that clarity is an action, not a feeling.

- Problemă vs. decizie vs. direcție (15 min)
- Costul amânării și costul deciziei greșite (10 min)
- Când claritatea înseamnă „nu știm încă" (15 min)

### Module 2 — Instrumente de prioritizare (90 min)
- Matricea Eisenhower (urgent/important)
- Matricea impact/efort
- Lista MUST, SHOULD, COULD
- Exercițiu: aplicarea pe lista personală sau de echipă

### Module 3 — Cadre de luare a deciziilor (90 min)
- Procesul decizional în 6 pași
- Decizii individuale vs. decizii de echipă
- Criterii decizionale: cost, risc, valoare, aliniere, timp
- Exercițiu: decizie reală cu cadru ales

### Module 4 — Schimbarea și rezistența (60 min)
- Modelul de schimbare simplificat: starea prezentă → starea dorită → gap → pași
- Surse comune de rezistență: pierdere, frică, oboseală, lipsă de încredere
- Cum să implici oamenii fără să cedezi calitatea deciziei
- **Change Mapping exercise (40 min):** Participants use the Change Map template to map a real or realistic change situation. Deliverable: completed Change Map with current state, desired state, gap, first 3 steps and top 2 resistance points.

**Boundary with other PMDs:** This module does **not** teach communication skills (PMD_002) or conflict de-escalation (PMD_003). It assumes participants can use existing skills to engage others; the focus here is on the change framework itself.

### Module 5 — Comunicarea direcției (60 min)
- Cum să spui „încotro ne îndreptăm" într-un mod care aliniază
- Mesajul de direcție: de unde, încotro, de ce, ce se schimbă, ce rămâne
- Exercițiu: 3-minute direction pitch using the Direction Pitch template

**Boundary with PMD_002:** This module focuses on the content and structure of direction, not on active listening, feedback or communication mechanics. Those belong to PMD_002.

### Module 6 — Planul de 30 de zile (30 min)
- Translatarea deciziei în acțiuni
- Identificarea priorităților, responsabililor și primilor pași
- Resurse de continuare

## 9. Assessment

### Formative assessment
- Observation of framework application
- Peer feedback on direction pitches
- Module-end quick checks

### Summative assessment
- **Practical deliverable:** One completed Stakeholder Map + Change Map + Direction Pitch + 30-day action plan.
- **Reflection brief:** 150–250 words on the most important clarity gained and how it will change the participant's next 30 days.

### Practical deliverable rubric — Decision and action plan

| Criterion | Excellent (4) | Good (3) | Acceptable (2) | Needs revision (1) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Problem clarity** | The real decision or direction is clearly named | Mostly clear | Vague | Missing |
| **Framework fit** | Chosen framework fits the situation | Reasonable fit | Forced fit | No framework |
| **Stakeholder / criteria map** | Key stakeholders and criteria identified with rationale | Most identified | Some identified | None identified |
| **Change analysis** | Change Map shows clear current/desired state, gap, steps and resistance | Mostly complete | Some elements missing | Incomplete |
| **Direction pitch** | Message is clear, compelling and actionable | Mostly clear | Vague | Confusing |
| **Action plan** | 30-day plan has priorities, owners and first steps | Plan present | Incomplete | No plan |

### Success indicators
- 100% of participants submit Stakeholder Map, Change Map, Direction Pitch and action plan.
- 90% of participants report increased clarity on at least one priority.
- 80% of participants identify at least 2 concrete actions they will take within 30 days.

### 10.1 Troubleshooting guide

| Issue | Solution |
|---|---|
| Participant says „nu am decizii de luat" | Reframe: direction, priority and trade-off are all forms of decision. |
| Group fixates on one problem | Use the 6-step process to broaden or narrow the frame. |
| Team has hidden conflict about priorities | Use impact/effort matrix visibly; do not resolve interpersonal conflict (PMD_003). |
| Participant wants to revisit past decisions | Acknowledge, then redirect to forward-looking action. |
| Online participants disengage | Use shorter activities, polls and breakout rooms. |

### 10.3 Decision Tools Quick Reference Card + Next-Day Clarity Sprint

**Decision Tools Quick Reference Card**

| Tool | Best for | Question it answers |
|---|---|---|
| Matricea Eisenhower | Sorting tasks by urgency/importance | „Ce fac mai întâi?" |
| Matricea Impact/Efort | Choosing initiatives | „Unde obținem cel mai mult cu resursele disponibile?" |
| MUST / SHOULD / COULD | Cutting a long list | „Ce este esențial vs. opțional?" |
| Procesul decizional în 6 pași | Complex decisions | „Ce decizie trebuie luată și cum?" |
| Change Map | Implementing change | „De unde plecăm, încotro mergem și ce trebuie să se schimbe?" |

**Next-Day Clarity Sprint**

Use this 15-minute sprint the next day after the program.

1. **Name it (2 min):** Write the decision, direction or priority you need to clarify.
2. **Map stakeholders (3 min):** Who is affected? Who has influence? Who can help?
3. **Choose a tool (2 min):** Which framework fits best?
4. **Generate 2–3 options (3 min):** Realistic alternatives.
5. **Pick criteria (2 min):** What matters most? (cost, time, risk, alignment, impact)
6. **Decide and draft the message (3 min):** What will you say and to whom?

### 10.7 Resistance Response Library

#### Education
- **Resistance:** „Nu avem timp pentru încă un proiect."
  **Response:** „Valid. De aceea folosim matricea impact/efort ca să vedem ce putem elimina, nu doar ce adăugăm."
- **Resistance:** „Părinții nu vor înțelege."
  **Response:** „Atunci mesajul de direcție trebuie să fie tradus pentru ei. Lucrăm la asta împreună."

#### Healthcare
- **Resistance:** „Schimbarea asta pune pacientul în pericol."
  **Response:** „Siguranța pacientului este primul criteriu. Dacă propunerea nu îndeplinește acest criteriu, nu merge mai departe."
- **Resistance:** „Colegii mei nu vor colabora."
  **Response:** „Identificăm stakeholderii și începem cu cei deschiși. Rezistența devine informație, nu barieră."

#### Public Administration
- **Resistance:** „Procedurile nu ne permit."
  **Response:** „Verificăm ce este constrângere reală și ce este presupunere. Aducem specialistul în proceduri în discuție."
- **Resistance:** „Cetățenii se vor plânge."
  **Response:** „Comunicăm transparent de ce se schimbă și ce beneficii aduce. Reclamația devine feedback."

#### Business
- **Resistance:** „Nu avem buget."
  **Response:** „Analizăm costul de a nu decide și prioritizăm ce poate fi făcut cu resursele actuale."
- **Resistance:** „Echipa este deja saturată de schimbări."
  **Response:** „Înțeleg oboseala. De aceea eliminăm ceva vechi înainte să adăugăm ceva nou."

#### NGO
- **Resistance:** „Acest grant ne-ar schimba misiunea."
  **Response:** „Verificăm alinierea cu misiunea ca prim criteriu. Dacă nu se aliniază, refuzăm politicos."
- **Resistance:** "Voluntarii nu vor fi de acord."
  **Response:** "Invităm voluntarii într-o conversație de clarificare înainte de a decide."

### 10.9 Pre-decision checklist

Before finalising a decision or direction, I confirm that:
- [ ] I have named the real problem, decision or direction.

## 11. Sector adaptations

### Education
- **Focus:** curriculum priorities, resource allocation, parent engagement strategies, team direction under inspection pressure.
- **Note:** educational leaders often balance statutory requirements, student needs and staff wellbeing.

#### Example scenario: Education
A school leadership team must decide which new initiative to prioritise this year: wellbeing programme, digital skills training or literacy intervention. All are valuable, but capacity is limited.

**Decision focus:** use impact/effort matrix and stakeholder criteria to make a transparent, defensible choice and communicate it clearly.

#### Advanced scenario: Education
A school must decide whether to restructure the timetable to allow more interdisciplinary projects. Teachers are split, parents are asking questions and the inspection is in 6 months.

**Leadership focus:** use stakeholder mapping, criteria analysis, change map and direction pitch to navigate competing interests and maintain educational quality.

### Healthcare
- **Focus:** patient flow decisions, resource allocation, service changes, team priorities under pressure.
- **Note:** clinical decisions remain with qualified professionals; this program supports operational and service-level decisions.

#### Example scenario: Healthcare
A clinic manager must decide whether to extend opening hours or add a new service line, given staff capacity and budget constraints.

**Decision focus:** apply criteria-based decision framework, assess risk and resistance, create a 30-day implementation plan.

#### Advanced scenario: Healthcare
A hospital department must decide how to reorganise patient flow after a sharp increase in admissions. Nurses, doctors and administration have different views.

**Leadership focus:** use decision criteria, stakeholder mapping and change map to co-create a direction that respects patient safety and staff capacity.

### Public Administration
- **Focus:** programme prioritisation, policy implementation, cross-department alignment, public service redesign.
- **Compliance note:** decisions must be transparent, evidence-based and aligned with institutional mandates.

#### Example scenario: Public Administration
A department must choose between digitising one of three services this year. Each has political, citizen and operational implications.

**Decision focus:** use multi-criteria analysis, map stakeholders and resistance, prepare a clear direction message.

#### Advanced scenario: Public Administration
A city hall must merge two departments to improve efficiency. Staff fear job losses and citizens fear reduced service quality.

**Leadership focus:** use change map, resistance response library and direction pitch to design and communicate the transition with transparency.

### Business / SMEs
- **Focus:** product/service priorities, market decisions, organisational change, team direction.
- **Note:** link clarity to business outcomes: faster decisions, better resource allocation, reduced drift.

#### Example scenario: Business
A founder must decide whether to enter a new market, improve the existing product or invest in sales. The team is divided.

**Decision focus:** apply decision framework, align criteria with business strategy, communicate direction and first steps.

#### Advanced scenario: Business
A mid-sized company must pivot its business model after losing a major client. The leadership team disagrees on whether to cut costs or invest in innovation.

**Leadership focus:** use prioritisation frameworks, decision criteria and direction pitch to align the team around a high-risk strategic choice.

### NGOs
- **Focus:** programme priorities, funding strategy, partnership decisions, mission alignment.
- **Note:** decisions must protect mission integrity and stakeholder trust.

#### Example scenario: NGO
An NGO leadership team must decide whether to accept a large restricted grant that would shift programme priorities for two years.

**Decision focus:** apply criteria including mission fit, operational capacity, risk and donor relationship, then prepare a transparent direction message.

#### Advanced scenario: NGO
An NGO must decide whether to merge with another organisation to survive a funding crisis. Staff and board have strong but different opinions.

**Leadership focus:** use decision-making frameworks, stakeholder map and change map to weigh mission, sustainability and people impact.

### 15.2 Facilitator requirements

**Essential:**
- Demonstrated experience in training adults in leadership, management or organisational development.
- Ability to guide groups through ambiguity without imposing answers.
- Familiarity with prioritisation and decision-making frameworks.
- Capacity to keep the program focused on leadership tools, not therapy or consulting.

**Desirable:**
- Background in education, healthcare, public administration, business or NGOs.
- Experience delivering both online and in-person experiential learning.
- Completion of the AnimaMinds Busola Deciziilor facilitator induction.

**Facilitator ratio:** 1 facilitator per 20 participants; 2 facilitators for groups over 20 or where team-level decisions are being worked.

### 15.3 Evaluation methodology

**Pre-program (T0):**
- Clarity questionnaire (5 items, Likert 1–5)
- Self-assessment of current decision-making habits

**During program:**
- Facilitator observation checklist
- Peer feedback on direction pitches and decision frameworks
- Module-end quick checks

**Post-program (T1, within 48h):**
- Repeat clarity questionnaire
- Stakeholder Map + Change Map + Direction Pitch + action plan submission
- Reflection brief
- Satisfaction survey (5 items)

**Follow-up (T2, 30 days):**
- Short online survey: decisions made, priorities clarified, actions taken
- Optional second reflection brief

### 15.4 Quality assurance process

1. **Content review cycle:** Program content is reviewed every 6 months or after significant updates in leadership or change-management research.
2. **Facilitator debrief:** After each delivery, facilitators complete a 15-minute debrief form noting what worked, what did not and any team dynamics.
3. **Participant feedback analysis:** Feedback scores and comments are reviewed after every 3 deliveries.
4. **Update log:** All changes to the PMD are logged with date, reason and approver.
5. **Sector validation:** Sector-specific scenarios are reviewed by at least one practitioner every 12 months.
6. **Safety audit:** Once per year, scenarios are reviewed to ensure they do not require therapy, consulting or organisational diagnosis.
