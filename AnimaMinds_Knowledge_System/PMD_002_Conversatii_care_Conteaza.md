## 1. Purpose

**Conversații care Contează** helps professionals communicate with clarity, courage and empathy in contexts where trust, collaboration and performance depend on the quality of dialogue.

The program builds practical communication skills that go beyond technique: listening, giving and receiving feedback, expressing disagreement constructively and aligning around shared goals.

---

## 2. Challenge solved

| Problem | Consequence |
|---|---|
| Feedback is avoided, delayed or delivered poorly | Low performance, resentment, disengagement |
| Teams talk past each other or work in silos | Misalignment, duplicated effort, conflict |
| Difficult conversations escalate or are postponed | Issues grow, relationships suffer |
| Communication focuses on persuasion rather than understanding | Superficial agreement, hidden resistance |
| Lack of psychological safety prevents honest dialogue | Innovation and learning are stifled |

The program solves these by developing communication as a daily practice, not a one-time technique.

---

## 3. Program promise

> Cuvintele potrivite schimbă totul.

**What "the right words" means in this program:**

Participants leave able to choose words that:
1. **Clarify** intentions and expectations rather than create ambiguity.
2. **Connect** people before correcting them.
3. **Invite** dialogue rather than shut it down.
4. **Build** trust incrementally, even in difficult moments.
5. **Produce** action and alignment, not just agreement.

Participants will leave the program able to:
- Prepare and deliver feedback that leads to change.
- Listen actively in emotionally charged conversations.
- Express disagreement without damaging the relationship.
- Align teams around decisions through dialogue.
- Maintain clarity and respect under pressure.

### Primary audience
- Profesioniști
- Manageri
- Profesori

### Secondary audience
- Lideri de echipă

- Funcționari publici (comunicare internă și externă)

### Not included
The program is **not** aimed at:
- Mediatori profesioniști sau terapeuți (prea de bază pentru ei)
- Persoane care caută doar tehnici de convingere sau manipulare
- Organizații care vor comunicare „de fațadă" fără schimbare reală

## 5. Learning outcomes

By the end of the program, participants will be able to:

1. **Explain** the difference between feedback, evaluation and appreciation.
2. **Prepare** a feedback conversation using a structured framework.
3. **Listen actively** in conversations where emotions or stakes are high.
4. **Express** disagreement or bad news without attacking the person.
5. **Align** a team around a decision using questions, not monologues.
6. **Repair** a working relationship after a difficult conversation.
7. **Apply** a checklist for psychologically safe communication.
8. **Create** a personal communication action plan for the next 30 days.

### Learning outcomes ↔ assessment matrix

| LO | Assessment method | Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| LO1 Explain feedback types | Short knowledge check + group discussion | Correct distinctions + examples |
| LO2 Prepare feedback | Feedback script worksheet | Completed script with situation, behaviour, impact, request |
| LO3 Listen actively | Role-play observation | Uses paraphrase, open questions, summarising |
| LO4 Express disagreement | Role-play deliverable | Disagreement expressed respectfully and clearly |
| LO5 Align a team | Team dialogue simulation | Shared decision recorded, all voices heard |
| LO6 Repair a relationship | Written reflection + optional role-play | Concrete repair steps identified |
| LO7 Apply safety checklist | Ethical/safety scenario cards | Checklist completed, risks identified |
| LO8 Create 30-day plan | 30-Day Action Plan template | Completed plan with first conversation defined |

## 7. Program structure

### Module 1 — Comunicarea care construiește (60 min)
**Practical hook (first 20 min):** Participants recall a recent conversation that went badly and identify the one sentence that changed everything. They rewrite that sentence using a simple reframing technique.

- De la monolog la dialog (15 min)
- Tipuri de feedback: apreciere, evaluare, coaching (15 min)
- Bariere în comunicare: frica, presupuneri, reputație (15 min)
- Introducere în siguranța psihologică (15 min)

### Module 2 — Ascultarea activă (60 min)
- Nivelurile ascultării: internală, focalizată, globală
- Tehnici: parafrază, clarificare, rezumare, validare emoțională
- Exercițiu: ascultare fără să întrerupi și fără să soliciți

### Module 3 — Feedback care produce schimbare (90 min)
- Structura feedbackului constructiv: situație, comportament, impact, cerere
- Feedback pozitiv și de îmbunătățire
- Exercițiu: scrierea unui mesaj de feedback în 4 propoziții
- Rol-play în perechi

### Module 4 — Conversații dificile (90 min)
- Cum să spui nu, cum să livrezi vești proaste, cum să gestionezi emoții
- Tehnica „Eu observ... Eu simt... Eu am nevoie..."
- Scenarii pe roluri: manager, coleg, profesor, specialist

**Boundary with Calm sub Presiune:** This module focuses on the *communication mechanics* of difficult conversations (clarity, respect, listening). It does **not** address conflict escalation, mediation or sustained pressure management. Those belong to PMD_004.

### Module 5 — Alinierea și decizia în echipă (60 min)
- Conversații pentru aliniere, nu pentru convinșere
- Întrebări puternice și ascultare în grup
- Tehnici: consens minus unu, maparea acordurilor

### Module 6 — Plan personal de comunicare (30 min)
- Identificarea a 3 conversații importante din următoarele 30 de zile
- Scopul, mesajul cheie și primul pas pentru fiecare
- Resurse de continuare

### Online adaptations
- Listening Triangles use breakout rooms with assigned roles.
- Team Alignment Map uses a shared digital whiteboard.
- Role-plays use video on/off or chat-based practice.

### Experience Edition adaptations
- Add an evening reflection on personal communication patterns.
- Include outdoor or movement-based listening exercise.
- Use longer, coached role-plays with peer feedback.

## 9. Assessment

### Formative assessment
- Observation of role-plays
- Peer feedback on feedback scripts
- Module-end quick checks

### Summative assessment
- **Practical deliverable:** One completed feedback script + one 4-sentence message ready to send.
- **Reflection brief:** 150–250 words on a recent conversation the participant will approach differently, and why.

### Practical deliverable rubric

| Criterion | Excellent (4) | Good (3) | Acceptable (2) | Needs revision (1) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Clarity** | Message is unambiguous and specific | Minor ambiguity | Vague or general | Confusing |
| **Connection** | Tone builds trust and openness | Mostly respectful | Neutral or slightly distant | Hostile or cold |
| **Actionability** | Clear request or next step | Next step implied | No clear next step | No request |
| **Psychological safety** | Recipient's perspective acknowledged | Some acknowledgment | Little acknowledgment | Dismissive |
| **Reflection** | Clear insight and planned change | Insight present | Surface reflection | No reflection |

### Success indicators
- 100% of participants submit a feedback script.
- 90% of participants report increased confidence in giving feedback.
- 80% of participants identify at least 2 important conversations they will initiate within 30 days.

### 10.1 Troubleshooting guide

| Issue | Solution |
|---|---|
| Participant is uncomfortable with role-play | Offer a written or observer role. Validate that discomfort is normal. |
| Group has existing conflict | Stay within program scope: communication mechanics, not mediation. Refer to PMD_004 if deep conflict exists. |
| Participant dominates discussions | Use structured turn-taking and breakout rooms. |
| Emotional content surfaces | Acknowledge it, allow a brief pause, and refocus on communication skills, not therapy. |
| Role-play feels artificial | Use participants' own anonymised cases with permission. |

### 10.2 Follow-up email sequence

**Day 2 — First conversation:**
> Subiect: Prima conversație importantă din planul tău
> Conținut: Care este prima conversație pe care o vei pregăti în această săptămână? Scrie într-un reply subiectul și mesajul cheie.

**Day 7 — Reflection:**
> Subiect: Ce a funcționat diferit în comunicarea ta?
> Conținut: Reflecție rapidă: ai folosit feedbackul constructiv? Ai ascultat activ? Ce ai schimba?

**Day 14 — Resource:**
> Subiect: 3 întrebări care transformă o conversație
> Conținut: Listă cu întrebări puternice pentru aliniere și clarificare.

**Day 30 — Outcome:**
> Subiect: Planul tău de 30 de zile — rezultate
> Conținut: Survey scurt: câte conversații ai abordat diferit? Ce impact ai observat?

### 10.4 Psychological safety checklist

Before entering a difficult or important conversation, I confirm that:
- [ ] I have a clear purpose for the conversation.
- [ ] I am focusing on one behaviour or situation, not the person's character.
- [ ] I am willing to listen at least as much as I speak.
- [ ] I am prepared to acknowledge my own part in the situation.
- [ ] I am choosing words that connect before correcting.
- [ ] I have a private, appropriate setting.
- [ ] I have allowed enough time for dialogue, not just delivery.
- [ ] I am prepared to ask "Ce auzi tu când spun asta?" and adjust.

## 11. Sector adaptations

### Education
- **Focus:** feedback to students, parent conversations, colleague collaboration, classroom dialogue.
- **Note:** communication must model the behaviour educators want students to learn.

#### Example: Education prompt / scenario
**Context:** A teacher needs to tell a parent that their child is falling behind without causing defensiveness.

**Feedback script:**
```
Situație: „Am observat rezultatele la testul de săptămâna trecută."
Comportament: „Ana a răspuns corect la primele 3 întrebări, apoi a lăsat restul nefinalizat."
Impact: „Mă îngrijorează că poate nu a înțeles instrucțiunile sau că a fost copleșită."
Cerere: „Aș vrea să discutăm cum putem susține acasă pregătirea pentru următorul test."
```

#### Example deliverable: Parent communication script
AI or peer support may be used to draft, but the final message must be reviewed for tone and accuracy by the teacher.

### Healthcare
- **Focus:** patient communication, handover conversations, team feedback, breaking bad news (basic level only).
- **Note:** this program does **not** replace clinical communication training; it supports everyday professional dialogue.

#### Example: Healthcare prompt / scenario
**Context:** A nurse needs to give feedback to a colleague who consistently arrives late for handover.

**Feedback script:**
```
Situație: „Am remarcat ultimele trei schimburi."
Comportament: „Ai ajuns după începerea raportului."
Impact: „Colegii trebuie să repete informațiile, ceea ce poate afecta siguranța pacientului."
Cerere: „Aș vrea să găsim împreună o soluție ca să începem raportul la timp."
```

#### Example deliverable: Team feedback script
Human review by the sender is essential; no patient data is shared during practice.

### Public Administration
- **Focus:** official correspondence, internal team dialogue, public consultations, cross-department collaboration.
- **Compliance note:** communication must remain neutral, respectful and aligned with institutional values.

#### Example: Public administration prompt / scenario
**Context:** A manager needs to align two departments with competing priorities around a shared deadline.

**Alignment dialogue:**
- „Care sunt cele mai importante 3 rezultate pentru fiecare departament?"
- „Unde se suprapun interesele noastre?"
- „Ce compromis realist putem accepta împreună?"
- „Cine comunică ce și când?"

#### Example deliverable: Alignment meeting plan
Includes agenda, key questions and shared decision record.

### Business / SMEs
- **Focus:** client communication, team feedback, performance conversations, negotiation, cross-functional alignment.
- **Note:** link communication to business outcomes: clarity saves time, trust reduces turnover.

#### Example: Business prompt / scenario
**Context:** A team leader needs to address a missed deadline without blaming the individual.

**Feedback script:**
```
Situație: „Proiectul X a fost livrat cu două zile întârziere."
Comportament: „Nu am primit un semnal de avertizare înainte de termen."
Impact: „Clientul a fost afectat, iar echipa a lucrat sub presiune."
Cerere: „Aș vrea să stabilim un checkpoint săptămânal astfel încât să identificăm riscurile mai devreme."
```

#### Example deliverable: Performance or client conversation script
Reviewed for tone, brand voice and commercial sensitivity.

### NGOs
- **Focus:** donor communication, volunteer coordination, partner dialogue, beneficiary-sensitive messaging.
- **Note:** align communication with mission and values; avoid donor-centric language that dehumanises beneficiaries.

### 15.2 Facilitator requirements

**Essential:**
- Demonstrated experience in training adults in communication or leadership.
- Strong facilitation skills for sensitive, emotionally loaded topics.
- Ability to maintain boundaries and keep conversations within the program scope.
- Familiarity with feedback models and psychological safety concepts.

**Desirable:**
- Background in education, healthcare, public administration or business.
- Experience delivering both online and in-person experiential learning.
- Completion of the AnimaMinds Conversații care Contează facilitator induction.

**Facilitator ratio:** 1 facilitator per 16 participants; 2 facilitators for groups over 16 or where emotional intensity is expected.

### 15.3 Evaluation methodology

**Pre-program (T0):**
- Confidence questionnaire (5 items, Likert 1–5)
- Self-assessment of current feedback and difficult-conversation habits

**During program:**
- Facilitator observation checklist
- Peer feedback on feedback scripts and role-plays
- Module-end quick checks

**Post-program (T1, within 48h):**
- Repeat confidence questionnaire
- Feedback script submission
- Reflection brief
- Satisfaction survey (5 items)

**Follow-up (T2, 30 days):**
- Short online survey: conversations initiated, relationships improved, challenges encountered
- Optional submission of a second feedback script

### 15.4 Quality assurance process

1. **Content review cycle:** Program content is reviewed every 6 months or after any significant research update on psychological safety or feedback models.
2. **Facilitator debrief:** After each delivery, facilitators complete a 15-minute debrief form noting what worked, what did not and any emotional intensity.
3. **Participant feedback analysis:** Feedback scores and comments are reviewed after every 3 deliveries to identify trends.
4. **Update log:** All changes to the PMD are logged with date, reason and approver.
5. **Sector validation:** Sector-specific examples are reviewed by at least one practitioner every 12 months.
6. **Safety audit:** Once per year, scenarios and role-play boundaries are reviewed to ensure they do not trigger unresolved trauma or require therapeutic intervention.
