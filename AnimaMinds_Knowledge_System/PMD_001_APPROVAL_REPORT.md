## Detailed changes

### 1. Module 1 shortened and made practical

**What changed:**
- Module 1 duration reduced from 90 minutes to 60 minutes.
- Added a hands-on opening activity in the first 20 minutes: participants open a free AI tool and generate a short apology email, then compare the AI draft with their own version.
- Rebalanced remaining time to keep ethics and limitations but start from experience, not theory.

**Why changed:**
- Review finding: Module 1 was at risk of being too theoretical.
- Practical hook increases engagement, reduces anxiety and demonstrates AI's real-world use from minute one.

**Review finding resolved:** Yes — finding 4 and priority action 1.

---

### 2. Learning Outcomes ↔ Assessment Matrix

**What changed:**
- Added a matrix in Section 5 that maps each of the 8 learning outcomes to a specific assessment method and the required evidence.

**Why changed:**
- Review finding: not all outcomes had explicit measurement methods.
- CPD accreditation requires clear evidence that each learning outcome has been achieved.

**Review finding resolved:** Yes — finding 2 and priority action 2.

---

### 3. CPD Annex added

**What changed:**
- Added Section 15 with four sub-sections:
  - 15.1 Learning hours and proposed CPD points per format
  - 15.2 Facilitator requirements (essential, desirable, ratio)
  - 15.3 Evaluation methodology (T0, during program, T1, T2)
  - 15.4 Quality assurance process (review cycle, debrief, feedback analysis, update log, sector validation, privacy audit)

**Why changed:**
- Review finding: CPD readiness was the weakest area (score 5/10).
- Accreditation bodies require documented hours, qualified facilitators, structured evaluation and ongoing QA.

**Review finding resolved:** Yes — finding 9 and priority action 3.

---

### 4. Sector-specific examples

**What changed:**
- Expanded Section 11 to include, for each sector:
  - Focus areas
  - Privacy / compliance note
  - A concrete prompt example
  - An example deliverable
- Sectors covered: Education, Healthcare, Public Administration, Business, NGOs.

**Why changed:**
- Review finding: sector adaptations needed more depth and concrete examples.
- Practitioners need to see exactly how the program translates to their daily work.

**Review finding resolved:** Yes — finding 6 and priority action 4.

---

### 5. Missing resources added

**What changed:**
- Added a practical deliverable rubric in Section 9.
- Added troubleshooting guide in Section 10.1.
- Added 4-step follow-up email sequence in Section 10.2.
- Added participant 30-day action plan template in Section 10.3.
- Added ethical and privacy checklist in Section 10.4.
- Added backup plan for technical failures in technical requirements.

**Why changed:**
- Review finding: several supporting resources were missing, limiting facilitator effectiveness and participant follow-through.
- These resources support implementation, assessment and sustained behaviour change.

**Review finding resolved:** Yes — finding 8 and priority action 5.

---

### 6. Clarification of "maintaining control"

**What changed:**
- Added a dedicated subsection under Section 3 defining what control means in five specific behaviours:
  1. Decide which tasks to delegate.
  2. Verify facts and tone.
  3. Edit output to match voice and expertise.
  4. Protect sensitive data.
  5. Take responsibility for the final result.

**Why changed:**
- Review finding: the promise was clear but the meaning of "control" was abstract.
- Concrete definition makes the promise actionable and assessable.

**Review finding resolved:** Yes — finding 1 and priority action 6.

3. **Tool terms review:** The list of recommended free AI tools should be reviewed quarterly due to rapid changes in terms of service.
